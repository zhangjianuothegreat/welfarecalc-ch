In Switzerland, family allowances are regulated by law (FamZG). Our calculator uses the specific cantonal regulations of the Canton of Vaud (VD) for 2026 to determine your entitlement precisely. The Vaud algorithm is characterized by a global household index (ranking) and generous one-time allowances. Transparency is important to us.

1. Monthly Rates and One-Time Amounts (2026)

Allowance Type 1st & 2nd Child (Standard) From 3rd Child (Increased) Requirement / Age Limit
Child Allowance CHF 322 / Month CHF 365 / Month From the month of birth until the completed 16th year of age.
Education Allowance CHF 425 / Month CHF 468 / Month From the 16th up to max. 25th year of age (proof of education).
Disabled Children CHF 425 / Month CHF 468 / Month From the 16th up to max. 20th year of age (proof of illness/disability).
Birth Allowance CHF 1'617 (Single) / CHF 3'234 (per child for multiples) One-time for live birth or from the 24th week of pregnancy.
Adoption Allowance CHF 1'617 (Single case) / CHF 3'234 (per child for group adoption) One-time upon legal placement of minors for adoption.

2. The Precise Calculation Path (Core Logic)

1

Determination of the Category

The algorithm checks the age and employment or health status of each child:

  • 0 to 16 years: Assignment to the Child Allowance category.
  • 16 to 25 years: Assignment to the Education Allowance category, provided valid proof of education exists and the statutory income limit is not exceeded.
  • 16 to 20 years (Disabled): Assignment to the Disabled Children category (entitlement amount corresponds to the education allowance).
2

Determination of the Global Household Ranking (Rang de l'enfant)

Critical Algorithm Factor: The tiering from the 3rd child is calculated in the Canton of Vaud not separately by category. A global list of all allowance-entitled children in the household is created and sorted by age in descending order.

Household_Index_Assignment:
Sort_Children_by_Age(Descending);
Child_1 (Oldest) → Index 1 (Standard rate)
Child_2           → Index 2 (Standard rate)
Child_3           → Index 3 (Increased rate from 3rd child)
Child_n           → Index n (Increased rate from 3rd child)

This ensures that, for example, a third child who is in education receives the full increased rate of CHF 468, even if the first two younger children only receive child allowances.

3

Calculation of the Monthly Amount

The mathematical sum is formed dynamically based on the determined indices:

Monthly_Total_Entitlement:
Monthly_Total_Entitlement = ∑ [ Rate ( Category, Index ) ]
Where:
• If Index ≤ 2:
Child Allowance = 322 CHF | Education / Disabled = 425 CHF
• If Index ≥ 3:
Child Allowance = 365 CHF | Education / Disabled = 468 CHF
4

Calculation of One-Time Allowances (Birth / Adoption)

The algorithm checks whether a birth or adoption took place in the calculation year, and strictly differentiates between single and multiple births:

One_Time_Allowances_Total:
One_Time_Allowances_Total = Number_Children × One_Time_Amount_Per_Child
Parameters:
• Single (Number_Children == 1):
One_Time_Amount_Per_Child = 1'617 CHF
• Multiples (Number_Children ≤ 2):
One_Time_Amount_Per_Child = 3'234 CHF (per child)

Example: Twin birth → 2 × 3'234 CHF = 6'468 CHF Total.
Single → 1 × 1'617 CHF = 1'617 CHF Total.

5

Determination of the Annual Entitlement

The aggregation to the total annual entitlement is:

Annual_Amount_Calculation:
Annual Amount = ( Monthly_Total_Entitlement × 12 ) + One_Time_Allowances_Total

3. Defensive Validation and Algorithmic Limits

Integrated Safety Barriers (Anti-Error Validation):
  • Income limit for children in education: If a child in education (16-25 years) earns a personal taxable employment income of more than CHF 30'240 / year (or CHF 2'520 / month), the allowance entitlement expires immediately in the core engine algorithm (result = 0).
  • Simultaneous receipt prohibition (Exclusion principle): For the same child, a child allowance and an education allowance can never be granted simultaneously.
  • Age limit capping: The entitlement to child allowance expires at the end of the month in which the child reaches the age of 16. The entitlement to education allowance expires the day after the 25th birthday or upon early completion of education.
Entitlement according to federal law:
Only one parent can receive the allowance. A statutory priority order according to Art. 12 FamZG applies if both parents are employed (e.g., primarily the one with the main employment in the child's canton of residence).
