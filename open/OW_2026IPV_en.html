Trust through transparency. Here we disclose how our calculator determines your potential premium reduction (IPV) in the Canton of Obwalden (OW). This algorithm is strictly based on the legal guidelines of the Ausgleichskasse Obwalden (AKOW) for the year 2026.

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Progressive Self-Retention

From 9.50% Base Rate

Increases by 0.01% per CHF 100 of countable income above CHF 35,000.

Asset Surcharge

10.0%

Portion of taxable assets added to income.

2. Calculation Methodology

1

Countable Income

First, we determine your economic capacity based on cantonal deductions:

Countable Income = (Gross income - Total deductions) + 10% of taxable assets
Category / Deduction Type Allowance / Deduction (2026)
Married/Registered partner deduction CHF 7,000
Deduction per child / youth in education CHF 7,000
Permissible statutory deductions Professional expenses, maintenance contributions, insurance deductions, medical costs, etc.
Minimum income value CHF 0 (Negative values are set to 0)
2

Cantonal Income Limits (Thresholds)

If the countable income exceeds the following thresholds, there is no legal entitlement to IPV:

Household Type Income Limit (2026) Entitlement Status if Exceeded
Single persons / couples without children CHF 50,000 No entitlement (Subsidy = 0)
Families / single parents with children CHF 75,000 No entitlement (Subsidy = 0)
3

Total Standard Premiums 2026

The Canton of Obwalden defines annual reference values (standard premiums) for compulsory health insurance:

Age Group / Status Standard Premium (2026)
Adults (aged 26 and above) CHF 5,018.40 / year
Young adults (19-25 years, in education) CHF 3,570.00 / year
Children (0-18 years) CHF 1,380.00 / year
4

Progressive Self-Retention & Theoretical Entitlement

The reasonable own contribution (self-retention) increases progressively with rising income:

If income ≤ CHF 35,000:
Rate = 9.50%

If income > CHF 35,000:
Rate = 9.50% + (Floor((Income - 35,000) / 100) × 0.01%)

Self-Retention = Countable Income × Rate
Theoretical Entitlement = Max(0, Total Standard Premiums - Self-Retention)
5

Legal Minimum Guarantees (Minimum Subsidy)

To specifically relieve families and young adults, fixed minimum guarantees apply below certain income thresholds:

Target Group Condition (Countable Income) Guaranteed Minimum Subsidy
Children (1st to 3rd child) Below CHF 50,000 80% of child standard premium (CHF 1,104 / child)
Children (from 4th child onward) Below CHF 50,000 100% of child standard premium (CHF 1,380 / child)
Young adults (in education) Below CHF 25,000 50% of youth standard premium (CHF 1,785 / person)
Calculated Entitlement = Max(Theoretical Entitlement, Legal Minimum Guarantee)
6

Final Limitation and Disbursement

The final IPV payment amount is limited as follows:

• Annual Benefit = Min(Calculated Entitlement, Actual Health Insurance Premium)
• Monthly Benefit = Annual Benefit / 12 (rounded to the nearest franc)

Note: In the Canton of Obwalden, the benefit is typically paid directly via settlement with your health insurance company.

Special features for entitlement year 2026:
• 13th AHV pension: According to cantonal ordinance, the 13th AHV old-age pension paid out from 2026 onwards has no negative impact on IPV entitlement.
• Hypothetical income: For insured persons with a voluntary part-time workload, the income for determining need may be extrapolated to a hypothetical full-time workload (according to revised provisions as of 2026).
