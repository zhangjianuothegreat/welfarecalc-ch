2. Precise Calculation Path

1

Regional assignment (rent regions)

The Canton of St. Gallen is divided into 3 regions according to federal law. Our algorithm assigns the household budget based on the postal code (PLZ):

  • Region 1 (High): Major centers (e.g., City of St. Gallen, Rapperswil-Jona)
  • Region 2 (Medium): Suburban areas and medium-sized municipalities
  • Region 3 (Low): Rural communities (standard fallback if PLZ missing)
2

Recognized expenses (Needs)

The mathematical sum of all legally recognized expense items is calculated as follows:

Total Needs = Basic Living Expenses + Recognized Housing Costs + Health Insurance Premium

Recognized Housing Costs (Maximum Rent 2026):

1 person CHF 18,900 (1,575/month) CHF 18,300 (1,525/month) CHF 16,680 (1,390/month)
2 persons CHF 22,320 (1,860/month) CHF 21,720 (1,810/month) CHF 20,160 (1,680/month)

*Note for home ownership: For owner-occupied properties, actual mortgage interest and a maintenance allowance are recognized up to the respective regional maximum rent.

Recognized Health Insurance Premiums:

The algorithm caps the premium according to the cantonal guidelines of the SVA SG. The formula is:

Recognized HI Premium = Min(Actual Premium, Regional Standard Premium)
Regional Standard Premium (per year) Region 1 Region 2 Region 3
Adults (26+) CHF 6,564 CHF 6,144 CHF 5,808
Young adults (11-25) CHF 4,752 CHF 4,536 CHF 4,344
Children (0-10) CHF 1,488 CHF 1,464 CHF 1,380
3

Countable Income

Your income is weighted according to the strict provisions of the ELG (Supplementary Benefits Act):

  • Pensions & 13th AHV neutralization: Pension income is calculated on a 12-month basis. The newly introduced 13th AHV pension as of 2026 is mathematically fully neutralized and does not enter the calculation.
  • Earned income: An allowance is granted (CHF 1,300 for single persons / CHF 1,950 for married couples). Only 2/3 of the remaining earned income is counted as income.
  • Asset depletion: If net assets exceed the allowances, a portion is counted as imputed income.
    Allowances: CHF 30,000 (single persons), CHF 50,000 (married couples).
    Rate for nursing home residents: 1/5 per year.
    Rate for old-age pensioners living at home: 1/10 per year.
    Rate for IV (disability) pensioners living at home: 1/15 per year.
4

Result: EL Entitlement

The EL benefit closes the gap, provided all criteria are met:

Annual EL Benefit = Max(0, [Recognized Expenses] - [Countable Income])

The monthly payment amount equals the annual EL divided by 12, rounded commercially to the nearest franc.

Important: The Eligibility Threshold (Asset Threshold)
A legal entitlement to Supplementary Benefits exists only if the taxable net assets are below CHF 100,000 (for single persons) or CHF 200,000 (for married couples). The value of owner-occupied residential property is explicitly excluded from this threshold.
