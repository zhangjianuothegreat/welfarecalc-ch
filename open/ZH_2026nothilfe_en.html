Trust through transparency. Emergency aid for persons with a removal decision or in the asylum process is calculated in the Canton of Zurich according to clear, degressive criteria. Here we disclose the complete algorithm – based on official cantonal directives (2026) and federal law (SR 851.1).

1. Basic Emergency Aid Parameters (Zurich 2026)

Basic Daily Rate (Single Person)

CHF 10.25 / Day

Collective accommodation, cash amount including meal allowance & basic needs.

Couple Factor (Degression)

Factor 1.82

Second adult receives an additional 82% of the basic rate.

Child Supplement Factor

Factor 0.62

Per child: 62% of the basic rate.

Progression Bonus (from 3rd child)

+12% per additional child

Additional boost from the third child onwards (12% of the basic rate).

2. The Calculation Path (Step by Step)

1

Determination of the Adult Daily Rate (Degression Model)

First, the daily basic amount is determined based on the number of adults. The Canton of Zurich uses a couple factor of 1.82 (i.e., the second person receives an 82% surcharge):

Single person: CHF 10.25 per day
Multi-person household: 10.25 + (Adults - 1) × 10.25 × 0.82

For 2 adults, the daily rate is CHF 18.66, for 3 adults CHF 27.07, etc.

2

Child Supplements & Progression Bonus

For each child (< 18 years), a supplement of 62% of the basic rate is granted. From the third child onwards, an additional progression bonus of 12% of the basic rate per additional child applies to adequately support larger families.

Child base = Number of children × (10.25 × 0.62)
Bonus (from 3rd child) = (Children - 2) × (10.25 × 0.12)
3

Monthly Extrapolation & Rounding

Emergency aid is calculated based on 30.42 days (average month). The final amount is rounded commercially to the nearest whole franc according to cantonal standard (0 cent steps).

Monthly entitlement = ROUND( (Total daily amount × 30.42) ; 0 )
Rounding Rule ZH Example
Commercial rounding to 1 CHF CHF 247.83 → CHF 248.00
Round down below .50 CHF 312.49 → CHF 312.00
Special features in the Canton of Zurich (mixed housing):
• The pure cash emergency aid covers meals and immediate basic needs. Accommodation costs (collective accommodation) are billed directly with the operators and are not included in this cash amount.
• Asset allowances are based on EL/emergency aid practice (max. CHF 4,000 per person).
• Persons with a legally binding removal decision are entitled to emergency aid, not to ordinary social welfare (conforms to Art. 12 BV).
