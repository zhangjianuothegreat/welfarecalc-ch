In Switzerland, family allowances are regulated by law through the Federal Act on Family Allowances (FamZG). The Canton of Zug (ZG) grants cantonal allowances that exceed the federal statutory minimum rates. Our calculator precisely determines your entitlement according to the official Zug guidelines for the year 2026.

1. Monthly Rates ZG (2026)

Child Allowance

CHF 330 / Month

For children up to the completed 16th year of age (or 20th year of age in case of incapacity for work).

Education Allowance

CHF 330 to CHF 385

CHF 330 (15–18 years) and CHF 385 (from completed 18th up to max. 25th year of age).

2. The Official Calculation Method

1

Requirements & Income Limits

Before allowances are granted, the algorithm checks the legal hurdles:

  • Minimum income of parents: The applying parent must earn an AHV-liable income of at least CHF 630 per month (or CHF 7,560 per year) to be considered employed.
  • Income limit for children in education: If a child is in education and earns their own employment income (e.g., apprenticeship salary) of more than CHF 2,520 per month (CHF 30,240 per year), the entitlement to the education allowance is completely forfeited.
2

Determination of Category & Age Limits

The algorithm classifies each child based on age and education status:

Category & Status Age Range Monthly Amount
Child Allowance (Standard) 0 to completed 16th year of age CHF 330
Child Allowance (Incapacity for Work) 16 to completed 20th year of age CHF 330
Education Allowance (in Education) From 15th to completed 18th year of age CHF 330
Education Allowance (in Education) From 18th up to max. 25th year of age CHF 385

*Note: In the Canton of Zug, there is no increase or gradation from the 3rd child or by age within the child allowance (no automatic increase from age 12).

3

Calculation of the Monthly Total Amount

The monthly sum is determined by the precise addition of the qualified children:

Monthly Sum = (Number of Child Allowances & Education Allowances ≤ 18 × CHF 330) + (Number of Education Allowances > 18 × CHF 385)
4

One-time Allowances (Birth/Adoption)

In contrast to some other cantons (such as GE, VS or FR), the Canton of Zug grants no one-time birth or adoption allowances.

Allowance Type Amount (ZG)
Birth Allowance CHF 0
Adoption Allowance CHF 0
5

Determination of the Annual Entitlement

Payment is made monthly via the employer or the cantonal family compensation fund (AK Zug):

Annual Amount = (Monthly Total Entitlement) × 12
Entitlement & Priority Order:
Only one allowance is paid per child. If both parents work, the legal priority order according to Art. 12 FamZG regulates the primary claim (order: employed parent, custody, child's residence, higher AHV income).
