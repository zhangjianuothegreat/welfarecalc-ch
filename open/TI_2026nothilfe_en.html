Economic emergency aid (Soccorso d'emergenza) for individuals with a legally binding removal decision is measured in the Canton of Ticino according to clear legal criteria. Here we disclose the complete algorithm that underlies our calculation module – precisely tailored to the official practice in 2026.

1. Legal Parameters for Ticino (2026)

Base Daily Rate (1st Adult)

CHF 10.00 / Day

Basic needs for food, clothing, and personal hygiene of an individual.

Accompanying Adults (Degression)

CHF 7.50 / Day

Fixed amount for each additional adult person in the same household.

Child Allowance

CHF 5.70 / Day per Child

Fixed additional amount per minor (without progressive multiple-child bonuses).

2. The Official Calculation Method (Step by Step)

1

Determination of the Degressive Adult Daily Rate

Ticino applies an absolute fixed-price degression model to reflect economies of scale in shared households:

If Adults = 1: CHF 10.00 per day
If Adults > 1: CHF 10.00 + (Number of Adults - 1) × CHF 7.50 per day

Compared to purely percentage multipliers, this absolute tiered approach represents a more precise approximation of the actual cantonal disbursement practice.

2

Add Child Allowances

For each child living in the household, a linear fixed amount of CHF 5.70 per day is added. According to the restrictive emergency aid guidelines, artificial progression (bonus from the 3rd child onwards) does not apply in the emergency aid context.

Daily Child Amount = Number of Children × CHF 5.70
Daily Total Amount = Adult Daily Rate + Daily Child Amount
3

Monthly Projection & Mathematical Rounding

The accumulated daily rate is projected to a monthly basis using the standardized annual average of 30.42 days. The final amount is rounded to the nearest whole franc according to standard commercial rounding rules.

Monthly Entitlement = ROUND( (Daily Total Amount × 30.42) ; 0 )
Special Features of Emergency Aid in the Canton of Ticino:
• In-kind benefit priority: Accommodation (assignment to a cantonal collective center / Centro collettivo) as well as the mandatory KVG health insurance are covered directly by the canton as in-kind benefits (Sachleistungen) and are not included in the cash amount.
• Subsidiarity: Own financial resources (asset allowance max. CHF 5,000) must be fully exhausted before a claim can be made.
• Disbursement mode: Although the mathematical projection is done over 30.42 days, local offices reserve the right to disburse the funds daily or weekly via vouchers.
