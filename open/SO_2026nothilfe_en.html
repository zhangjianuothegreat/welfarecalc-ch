Emergency aid (assistance in emergency situations) for persons required to leave the country or persons with a legally binding removal decision is calculated in the canton of Solothurn according to strict legal criteria. Here we disclose the complete mathematical model and algorithm for the year 2026.

1. Basic Emergency Aid Parameters (SO 2026)

Base Daily Rate (Adult)

CHF 8.00 / Day

Cash allowance/pocket money for a single person in a collective accommodation.

Couple Factor (Degression)

Factor 1.68

Two adult persons in one support unit receive together 168% of the individual rate.

Child Supplement Factor

Factor 0.52

Additional amount per child (52% of the adult base rate), independent of the number of children.

2. The Calculation Path (Step by Step)

1

Determination of the Adult Daily Rate

The canton of Solothurn uses a depressive scaling model for households with multiple adults:

If Adults = 1: CHF 8.00 / Day
If Adults > 1: CHF 8.00 + (Number of Adults - 1) × 8.00 × 1.68

Note: For a married couple/partnership, the second adult person receives a depressive rate of CHF 13.44 per day.

2

Add Child Supplement

For each child living in the same household, a flat-rate amount is added, corresponding to 52% of the adult base rate (equivalent to CHF 4.16 per child/day):

Daily Child Amount = Number of Children × (8.00 × 0.52) = Number of Children × CHF 4.16 / Day

The canton of Solothurn does not grant any additional progression or large-family bonus from the 3rd child within emergency aid (factor remains fixed at 0.52).

3

Monthly Extrapolation & Rounding

The total daily amount is extrapolated using the standardized Swiss monthly factor of 30.42 days. The result is commercially rounded to the next full franc (1.00 CHF), in accordance with cantonal enforcement practice for the disbursement of emergency aid credits.

Monthly Entitlement = ROUND( (Total Daily Amount × 30.42) ; 0 )
Important Administrative Particularities in Canton SO:
• Priority of Benefits in Kind: Emergency aid is primarily provided in the form of benefits in kind (accommodation, basic medical care, direct meals). The calculated amount represents the minimum pocket money/cash.
• Enforcement of Removal: Persons with a legally binding removal decision are, by law, no longer entitled to ordinary social welfare (SKOS), but fall exclusively into this minimal emergency system.
• Subsidiarity: Own financial resources (assets/income) must be completely exhausted before entitlement to cantonal emergency aid.
