How is social assistance calculated in the Canton of Appenzell Ausserrhoden? To ensure maximum transparency, we disclose the algorithm behind welfarecalc.ch. It is based on the Social Assistance Act (SHG AR) and the latest SKOS guidelines 2026.

1. Asset Check (The Initial Hurdle)

Allowance for Single Person

CHF 6,000

Plus CHF 3,000 per child/person in education.

Allowance for Couples

CHF 12,000

The maximum total allowance is capped at CHF 15,000 per household.

2. Calculation Method (Monthly)

1

Basic Living Expenses (BLE)

The BLE covers costs for food, clothing, energy, and household items (SKOS 2026):

Household SizeAmount per Month (2026)

Each additional person+ CHF 216
2

Housing & Health Costs

Recognition of actual costs up to the respective municipal maximum:

Recognized Need = Rent (Gross) + Health Insurance (Basic KVG coverage)

*Important: Maximum rent limits vary by municipality in Canton AR. Our calculator uses weighted average values (e.g., approx. CHF 1,050 for 1 person), unless a specific municipality is selected.

3

Earned Income Allowance (EIA) & Integration

To encourage the return to employment, Canton AR grants an allowance on net income:

EIA (Work Incentive) = Depends on workload and income (max. CHF 400 per person)

Additionally, integration bonuses (IZU) may be granted for non-employed individuals showing exceptional commitment.

4

Result: Monthly Entitlement

The difference calculation determines the monthly support amount:

Entitlement = (Basic Needs + Housing + Health) - (Net Income - EIA)
Special feature in Canton AR:
The Canton of Appenzell Ausserrhoden places great emphasis on subsidiarity. This means that before receiving benefits, all other claims (such as alimony, pensions, or housing subsidies) must be asserted.
