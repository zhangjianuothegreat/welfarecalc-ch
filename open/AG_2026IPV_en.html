Trust through transparency. Here we disclose how our calculator determines your potential premium reduction (IPV) in the Canton of Aargau. This algorithm is strictly based on the legal regulations of the SVA Aargau.

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Deductible (Self-contribution rate)

17.5 %

Share of income considered reasonable for insurance costs.

Asset consumption rate

20.0 %

Share of net assets added to income.

2. Calculation Steps

1

Relevant Income (LNA)

First, we determine your economic capacity:

[Taxable Income] + [20% of Net Assets] - [Allowance]
CategoryAllowance (Deduction)
Single individualsCHF 8'500
Single-parent familiesCHF 12'200
Married couplesCHF 8'000
Per child / young adult+ CHF 2'500
2

Total Standard Premiums 2026

Standard premiums are the benchmark values set by the canton for insurance costs:

AdultsCHF 5'830 / year
Young adults (19-25)CHF 4'260 / year
Children (0-18)CHF 1'380 / year
3

Calculating the Entitlement

The formula is:

Entitlement = [Total Standard Premiums] - ([LNA] × 17.5%)

The entitlement is limited by the actual premium paid.

Observe the asset limit:
If net assets exceed CHF 100,000 (single individuals) or CHF 200,000 (married couples), there is no legal entitlement to IPV, regardless of income.
