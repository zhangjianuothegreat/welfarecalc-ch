In the canton of Neuchâtel (NE), the amount of family allowances follows the federal cascade principle (allowance progression from the 3rd child onwards). Our algorithm does not treat blended families in isolation but precisely determines the entitlement based on the age order of all eligible children.

1. Legal Monthly Rates & One-Time Payments (2026)

2. Precise Calculation Method (Cascade Model)

1

Categorization & Validation

Each child is assigned to a category based on age and educational status:

  • 0 – 16 years: Basic child allowance.
  • 16 – 25 years: Education allowance (proof of training/university required).
  • Disabled children: Entitled to child allowance up to completed age 20.
Regulatory income threshold (2026): If a child in education earns their own employment income exceeding CHF 2'520 / month (CHF 30'240 / year), the entitlement to the education allowance is completely forfeited.
2

Chronological Sorting (Cascade Ranking)

To avoid errors in blended families (children + adolescents in education), all eligible children of the household are sorted together by date of birth (from oldest to youngest child). The sequential index determines the child's cascade rank:

Rank 1 (oldest child): Base rate (CHF 240 or CHF 320) Rank 2 (second oldest child): Base rate (CHF 240 or CHF 320) Rank 3+ (from third child): Increased rate (CHF 270 or CHF 350)
3

Calculation of Monthly Amount

The algorithm iterates over the sorted array and sums individual entitlements:

Total monthly entitlement = Σ Individual allowance (Category, Rank)

Comparative example for a blended family (3 children)

Scenario: 1 child in education (the oldest) and 2 younger school-aged children.

Child (by age) Type Cascade Rank Calculated Entitlement
Child 1 (oldest) Education allowance Rank 1 CHF 320 (Base education)
Child 2 (middle) Child allowance Rank 2 CHF 240 (Base child)
Child 3 (youngest) Child allowance Rank 3 CHF 270 (Increased from 3rd child)
Total monthly entitlement in the correct cascade model: CHF 830 / month

*Note: An incorrect, isolated model would mistakenly classify Child 3 as "1st child in the child allowance category" and allocate only CHF 240 (loss of CHF 30/month for the family).

4

One-Time Allowances & Annual Entitlement

The one-time cantonal allowances are added directly to the annual entitlement:

Annual amount = (Total monthly entitlement × 12) + (Number of birth allowances) + (Number of adoption allowances)
Eligibility & Income Requirement:
To be eligible in Canton Neuchâtel, the entitled parent, whether an employee or self-employed, must earn a minimum AHV-contributory income of at least CHF 630 / month (CHF 7'560 / year). Furthermore, in case of competing claims from both parents, the statutory priority order according to Art. 12 FamZG applies. This model applies to non-agricultural employees and self-employed persons.
