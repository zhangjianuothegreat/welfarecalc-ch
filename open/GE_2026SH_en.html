How is social welfare calculated in the Canton of Geneva? To ensure absolute transparency, we disclose the complete algorithm behind welfarecalc.ch. The calculations are based on the guidelines of Hospice Général as well as the SKOS directives for the year 2026.

1. Asset Check (The Entry Hurdle)

Allowance for single person

CHF 6'000

Plus CHF 3'000 per child or person in education/training.

Allowance for couples

CHF 12'000

Maximum total allowance capped at CHF 15'000 per household.

2. Calculation Pathway (Monthly)

1

Basic living needs (GBL)

The GBL 2026 in the Canton of Geneva applies the specific equivalence scale:

Household constellation Amount per month (2026)
Single person CHF 1'061
Couple / 2 persons CHF 1'624
Additional adult CHF 400
Child (under 11 years) CHF 391
Child (11-15 years) CHF 699
Child (> 15 years / in education) CHF 1'061
2

Housing costs & Health

Recognition of actual costs up to the cantonal maximum of Hospice Général:

Recognized need = Rent (up to maximum cap) + Health insurance (KVG - IPV)

*Rent ceilings in Geneva are tiered: e.g., approx. CHF 1'400 for 1 person, CHF 1'600 for 2 persons.

3

Income crediting & Employment allowance

To promote professional integration, part of the income is not counted:

Attributable income = Net income - allowance (20% of salary)

Note: Geneva also grants an integration bonus (Indemnité d'incitation) of approx. CHF 100 for those in employment.

4

Result: Monthly entitlement

The coverage of needs is calculated as the difference between total requirement and income:

Entitlement = (GBL + Rent + Health insurance + Allowances) - (Attributable income)
Special feature of Canton Geneva:
If asset allowances are exceeded, Hospice Général may include a staggered asset amortisation (amortissement) over a period of up to 10 years in the calculation.
