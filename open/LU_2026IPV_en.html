Trust through transparency. Here we disclose how our calculator determines your entitlement in the Canton of Lucerne. The algorithm strictly follows the guidelines of the Lucerne Compensation Office (WAS).

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Asset Surcharge

10.0 %

Percentage of taxable assets added to income.

Minimum Subsidy

CHF 100 / year

The calculated amount must reach this threshold to be paid out.

2. Calculation Pathway

1

Relevant Income (LNA)

The LNA (Lucerne Net Adjustment) determines your subsidy level:

LNA = [Annual net income] + [10% of taxable assets]
Asset Threshold (Hard Cap):
Individuals: > CHF 100,000
Married couples/Partners: > CHF 200,000
If exceeded, no entitlement regardless of income.
2

Determining the Subsidy Rate

Based on your LNA, a percentage is applied to the reference premiums:

CHF 30,00080 %
CHF 50,00070 %

Above CHF 130,0010 %
3

Reference Premiums 2026 (Region 2)

The calculation is not based on your actual premium but on cantonal standard values:

AdultsCHF 5,304 / year
Young adults (in education)CHF 3,780 / year
Children (0-18)CHF 1,224 / year

*Values for Region 2 (standard). Regions 1 and 3 differ slightly.

4

Application of the Minimum Guarantee

A protection mechanism applies for low-income families:

Guarantee = (Children × Ref-Premium × 80%) + (Young adults × Ref-Premium × 50%)

Our calculator automatically selects the higher amount between Standard Subsidy and Minimum Guarantee.

5

Final Entitlement

IPV Entitlement = Min([Calculated amount], [Actual premium])
