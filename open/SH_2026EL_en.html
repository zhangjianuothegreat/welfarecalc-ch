2

Countable Income

All income is assessed according to legally compliant EL rules:

  • Pension benefits (AHV/IV): 12 months of regular pension payments. The statutory 13th AHV pension is fully neutralized in the EL calculation according to federal resolution and is not counted as income.
  • Earned income: Adjusted for the statutory allowance (CHF 1,500 for singles / CHF 2,250 for married couples). Only 2/3 of the income exceeding the allowance is counted as income.
  • Asset depletion: A fixed portion of net assets after deducting the allowance:
    Asset depletion = max(0, (Net assets - Asset allowance) / Divisor)
    Divisor: Old-age pensioners (AHV) = 10 | Disability pensioners (IV) = 15.

3

Result: Statutory EL Entitlement

EL covers the mathematical gap between your recognized needs and countable income:

Annual EL Benefit = [Recognized Expenditure] - [Countable Income]

The monthly payment amount equals the annual EL benefit divided by 12, rounded commercially to the nearest franc.

Important: The Asset Threshold (Eligibility Threshold)
A statutory entitlement to Supplementary Benefits exists only if the taxable net assets are below CHF 100,000 (for single persons) or CHF 200,000 (for married couples). The value of owner-occupied residential property is not counted towards this eligibility threshold.
