Trust through transparency. Here we disclose how our calculator determines your potential premium reduction in the canton of Solothurn. This algorithm is strictly based on the legal foundations (BGS 831.1) of the Solothurn Compensation Office (AKSO).

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Progressive Deductible

9.0 % — 15.0 %

Depending on the adjusted net income (RDU).

Asset Conversion

5.0 %

Portion of taxable assets after deduction that is attributed to income.

2. The Calculation Path

1

Relevant Net Income (LNA)

First, the economic capacity is determined including assets:

[Net Income] + [5% × (Taxable Assets - Allowance)]

Note: Depending on marital status, the canton provides a deduction on taxable assets before the 5% conversion applies.

2

Deductions and Adjusted Income (RDU)

Social and child deductions are subtracted from the LNA to obtain the adjusted net income (RDU):

RDU = Max(0, LNA - Total Deductions)
Category Deduction Amount (annual)
Single persons CHF 12,000
Married couples / Cohabiting couples CHF 18,000
Per child / Young adult in education + CHF 6,000
3

Official Reference Premiums 2026 (annual)

The standardized annual reference premiums set by the Government Council for the year 2026 are:

Category Monthly Annual (Calculation Basis)
Adults (26 years and older) CHF 422 CHF 5,064
Young Adults (19-25, in education) CHF 305 CHF 3,660
Children (0-18) CHF 98 CHF 1,176
4

Progressive Deductible Rate

The reasonable own contribution from income increases linearly between an RDU of CHF 40,000 and CHF 120,000:

If RDU ≤ 40,000: Rate = 9%
If RDU ≥ 120,000: Rate = 15%
In between: Rate = 9% + 6% × ((RDU - 40,000) / 80,000)
Own Contribution (CHF) = RDU × Deductible Rate
5

Subsidy Determination & Minimum Guarantee

The provisional subsidy results from the reference premiums minus the own contribution. At the same time, the statutory minimum guarantee for children and young people applies:

Provisional Subsidy = Max(0, [Total Reference Premiums] - [Own Contribution])

Regardless of income, the following minimum shares of the reference premium are guaranteed:

Beneficiary Category Guaranteed Share
Children (Minimum Reduction) 80 % of the reference premium (CHF 940.80 / year)
Young Adults in Education 50 % of the reference premium (CHF 1,830.00 / year)
Entitlement = Max(Provisional Subsidy, Statutory Minimum Guarantee)
6

Payout Limits

Finally, the calculated entitlement is checked against the administrative minimum:

If Annual Subsidy < CHF 240: Payout = 0

*The entitlement is also capped by the effective premium paid by the insured person.

Important Income and Asset Limits:
If the relevant net income (LNA) exceeds the amount of CHF 80,000, there is generally no entitlement to IPV in the canton of Solothurn (entry threshold), unless special hardship cases or a high number of children exist.
