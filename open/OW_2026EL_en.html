2. Precise Calculation Path

1

Recognized Expenditure (Needs)

The calculator aggregates all legally defined expenditure items:

Recognized Expenditure = Basic Needs + Recognized Rent + Health Insurance Premium Allowance

Health Insurance Standard Premiums 2026 (Canton OW)

The algorithm uses, according to cantonal law, the minimum of the actual declared premium and the cantonal standard premium :

Recognized HI premium = Math.min(Actual premium, Cantonal standard premium)

2

Countable Income

The modified income components are compared to the expenditure:

  • Pensions & basic income: Regular pension amounts (AHV/IV). Important: The 13th AHV pension, mandated by popular initiative, is neutralized by federal law for EL purposes. The calculator automatically deducts this: Countable = (Pension ÷ 13) × 12 .
  • Earned income: Reduced by the statutory allowance (CHF 1,300 single / CHF 1,950 married couples), of which only 2/3 is then counted as income.
  • Asset drawdown: Net assets minus allowance, divided by the exemption divisor:
Asset parameters (OW) Amount / Factor
Allowance for single persons CHF 30,000
Allowance for married couples CHF 50,000
Additional allowance per child CHF 15,000
Divisor (asset drawdown factor): Old-age pensioners (AHV) = 10 | Disability pensioners (IV) = 15.
3

Determining the EL Benefit Amount

The difference closes the financial coverage gap:

Annual EL Entitlement = Max(0, [Recognized Expenditure] - [Countable Income])

The monthly payment amount results from dividing the annual entitlement by 12, rounded commercially to the full Swiss franc.

Important: The statutory asset entry threshold
Following the 2021 reform, a general entitlement to EL exists only if the countable net assets (excluding the value of owner-occupied residential property) fall below the following limit:
  • Single person: CHF 100,000
  • Married couples: CHF 200,000
  • Additional per child: CHF 50,000
If this entry threshold is exceeded, the system automatically blocks the payment ( err_asset_exceeded_ow ).
