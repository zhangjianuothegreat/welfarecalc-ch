In line with our transparency philosophy, we disclose the mathematical foundation for the IPV calculator of the Canton of Jura. This algorithm is based on the Arrêté des Gouvernements jurassien of October 30, 2025.

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Asset Threshold

CHF 150,000

If exceeded, no entitlement to IPV in principle.

Asset Consumption (Imputation)

5.0 %

Percentage of net assets counted as virtual income.

2. Step-by-Step Calculation

1

Relevant Income (LNA)

The economic basis (LNA) is determined as follows:

[Taxable Income] + [5% Net Assets] - [Alimony Payments]
Income Threshold (LNA)Status
CHF 26,999Single / Without children
CHF 52,999Households with children
2

Basic Subsidy & Bonuses

The Canton of Jura combines a fixed basic subsidy with specific allowances:

  • Basic subsidy: Depending on the LNA bracket (max CHF 2,700/year).
  • Child bonus: CHF 1,200 / year per child.
  • Education bonus: CHF 2,352 / year per person (< 25 years).
3

Household Allowance (Linear Reduction)

For households with children and LNA < CHF 18,000, an extra amount is calculated:

Monthly allowance = 300 - (LNA / 18,000) × (300 - 15)

This amount is granted per parent and decreases linearly as income rises.

4

Entitlement Cap

Entitlement = Min([Basic subsidy + Bonuses + Allowance], [Actual premium])

For households with children, the minimum entitlement is CHF 120/year if a subsidy has been calculated.

Important note:
The values shown here are based on the cantonal ordinance for 2026. The LNA in the canton of Jura accounts for asset consumption more moderately (5%) than, for example, the canton of Aargau (20%).
