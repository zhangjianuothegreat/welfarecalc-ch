In Switzerland, family allowances are regulated by law (FamZG). Our calculator uses the specific cantonal rates of the canton of Uri for 2026 to precisely determine your entitlement. Transparency is important to us.

1. Monthly Rates & One-time Rates (2026)

Child Allowance

CHF 240 / Month

For children from 0 up to the completed 16th year of age.

Education Allowance

CHF 290 / Month

From 16 years until the end of education (max. 25).

Birth / Adoption Allowance

CHF 1'200 / One-time

One-time lump sum upon the birth or adoption of a child.

2. The Calculation Method

1

Determination of the Category

The algorithm checks the age and status of each child:

  • 0 - 16 years: Entitlement to child allowance (CHF 240).
  • 16 - 25 years: Entitlement to education allowance (CHF 290), provided education/studies are proven.
  • Disabled children: Child allowance up to the 20th year of age.
2

Calculation of the Monthly Amount

The monthly total is formed linearly from the respective individual rates without gradation based on the number of children:

Monthly Total = (Number of Children × CHF 240) + (Number in Education × CHF 290)

*Important: In the canton of Uri, there is no increase for large families (from the 3rd child) and no age levels within the same category.

3

One-time Allowances (Birth/Adoption)

In contrast to the cantons of Aargau or Appenzell Innerrhoden, the canton of Uri grants a statutory one-time birth or adoption allowance.

Allowance Type Amount (UR) Condition
Birth Allowance CHF 1'200 One-time per newborn
Adoption Allowance CHF 1'200 One-time per adopted child
One-time Sum = (Number of Newborns × CHF 1'200) + (Number of Adoptions × CHF 1'200)
4

Determination of the Annual Entitlement

The total annual entitlement in the calculated calendar year consists of the monthly payments and any one-time payments:

Annual Amount = (Monthly Total × 12) + One-time Sum
Entitlement & Priority Order:
According to FamZG Art. 12, only one person per child can receive the allowance. If both parents are employed or work in different cantons, the legal priority order regulates the primary entitlement. The other canton pays a differential allowance if its rates are higher.
