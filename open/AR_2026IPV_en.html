Transparency is the foundation of trust. On this page, we disclose the mathematical calculation logic behind our IPV calculator for the Canton of Appenzell Ausserrhoden. This algorithm follows the guidelines of SoVAR (Social Insurance Appenzell Ausserrhoden).

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Deductible Rate (self-retention)

46.0 %

Rate applied to the assessable income after deductions.

Asset Consumption Rate

15.0 %

Proportion of taxable assets added to income.

2. Calculation Path

1

Relevant Income (LNA)

The LNA (Relevant Income) determines your financial base burden:

[Taxable Income]
+ [15% of Taxable Assets]
+ [Adjustments] (e.g., property maintenance, excess 3a contributions)

*Adjustments are based on the cantonal IPV calculation information sheet 2026.

2

Income Ceiling Check

If the LNA exceeds these thresholds, there is no entitlement:

Household Type Income Limit (LNA)
Single (0 children) CHF 35'000
Married couple (0 children) CHF 55'000
Married couple (2 children) CHF 75'900

Asset ceiling: CHF 120'000 (single) / CHF 200'000 (couple).

3

Social Deductions & Self-Retention

Social deductions are subtracted from the LNA to determine the "assessable income":

Category Basic Deduction
Single (without children) CHF 20'670
Couples / Persons with children CHF 31'005
Per child / young person + CHF 2'000
4

Reference Premiums 2026

The Canton of AR defines the following reference premium costs:

Adults (age 26+) CHF 6'025.20 / year
Young adults (age 19-25) CHF 2'116.80 / year
Children (age 0-18) CHF 1'114.80 / year
5

Benefit Calculation

Entitlement = [Total Reference Premiums] - ([LNA - Deductions] × 46%)

The calculated entitlement is capped by the premiums actually paid.

Special feature in Canton AR:
In the Canton of Appenzell Ausserrhoden, taxable assets are weighted relatively strongly at a rate of 15%. Moreover, the income thresholds depend precisely on the number of children (LNA table).
