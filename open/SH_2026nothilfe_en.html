Trust grows through transparency. In the Canton of Schaffhausen (SH), emergency aid for persons required to leave Switzerland living in collective accommodation is based on a flat-rate per-capita model. Here, we disclose the official cantonal algorithm for 2026 to ensure maximum transparency.

1. Calculation Parameters (SH 2026)

Monthly Per-Capita Flat Rate

CHF 405.00 / month

Uniform fixed amount per person (regardless of age or family status) for accommodation in a collective facility.

Type of Accommodation

Collective Accommodation

Applies to persons in the asylum sector with a legally binding removal decision.

Degression Factor

None (Linear Model)

No reductions for couples or child-specific deductions.

2. Calculation Path (Step by Step)

1

Determine Household Size

The cantonal algorithm does not distinguish between adults and children for emergency aid in collective accommodation. All persons in the support unit count equally as individuals.

Total number of persons = Number of adults + Number of children
2

Multiply by the Monthly Flat Rate

The monthly total entitlement of the support unit is calculated by multiplying the number of persons by the official flat rate of CHF 405.00.

Monthly total amount = Total number of persons × CHF 405.00
3

Daily and Yearly Extrapolation (Optional)

For statistical or comparative purposes, the flat rate can be extrapolated to daily (division by the cantonal standard factor 30.42) or yearly values.

Daily guideline = Monthly total amount / 30.42
Yearly guideline = Monthly total amount × 12
Special features of the Canton of Schaffhausen:
• Flat-rate system: Instead of degressive daily rates, the Canton of SH uses a clear, monthly per-capita flat-rate system for collective accommodation.
• Preference for benefits in kind: Emergency aid serves to secure survival and is primarily provided in the form of benefits in kind; cash payments are strictly regulated.
• Legal basis: Law on Public Social Welfare and Social Institutions (SHEG) of the Canton of Schaffhausen.
