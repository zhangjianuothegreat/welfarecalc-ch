2. Calculation Methodology

1

Recognised Expenditure

First, we sum all legally recognised expenditures:

Basic needs + Gross rent (up to limit) + Average health insurance premium
Maximum rent 2026 (Category Region 2)Annual amount
Single personsCHF 18,300 (CHF 1,525/month)
Married couplesCHF 21,780 (CHF 1,815/month)

*Note: Slightly lower limits apply in rural regions (Region 3) of Aargau.

2

Assessable Income

Then, your income is offset against the expenditure:

  • Pensions: 12 months of AHV or IV (The 13th month pension is neutralised).
  • Asset depletion: (Net assets - allowance) / 10 (for old-age pensioners).
  • Earned income: Reduced by a standard statutory deduction.
Asset allowancesExemption amount
Single personsCHF 30,000
Married couplesCHF 50,000
Owner-occupied propertyCHF 112,500
3

Calculation of EL Benefit

The EL covers the gap between expenditure and income:

Annual EL = [Recognised Expenditure] - [Assessable Income]
Important: Asset threshold (Eligibility threshold)
As of 2021, entitlement to EL only exists if net assets are below CHF 100,000 (single persons) or CHF 200,000 (married couples). Owner-occupied properties are not counted in this calculation.
