How is social assistance calculated in Nidwalden? To strengthen user trust, we disclose the full algorithm behind welfarecalc.ch. It is based on the Social Assistance Law of the Canton of Nidwalden (SHG NW) and the SKOS guidelines 2026.

1. Asset Check (The Entry Threshold)

Allowance for Single Person

CHF 6'000

Plus CHF 3'000 per child or person in education/training.

Allowance for Married Couples

CHF 12'000

Maximum total allowance capped at CHF 15'000 per assistance unit.

2. Calculation Path (Monthly)

1

Basic Living Needs (BLN)

The BLN 2026 in Canton NW covers costs for food, clothing, and household management:

Household Size / Category Amount per Month (2026)
1 Person (single) CHF 1'061
2 Persons (couple) CHF 1'624
Additional adult in household CHF 400
Child under 11 years CHF 391
Child 11 to 15 years CHF 699
Child over 15 years / trainee CHF 1'061

The basic living needs are calculated additively based on the actual household composition.

2

Housing Costs (Rent Limits)

Recognition of actual rent costs including ancillary costs up to the cantonal maximum:

Household Size Maximum recognized gross rent
1 Person CHF 1'000
2 Persons CHF 1'200
3 Persons CHF 1'400
4 Persons CHF 1'600
5+ Persons CHF 1'800

*Note: Only the actual rent up to the upper limit is recognized as a need (higher costs are not considered).

3

Health Costs & Health Insurance (after IPV)

The monthly health insurance premium (basic KVG insurance) is recognized, whereby the Individual Premium Reduction (IPV) is deducted first:

Recognized health insurance costs = KVG premium (max. average premium) - IPV contribution
4

Income Recognition & Deductions

A work allowance is granted on earned income to maintain work incentives. This is standardly 20% of gross wages:

Countable income = Net earned income - work allowance (20%)

Other income (such as alimony, child allowances, or pensions) as well as residual IPV and supplementary benefits (EL) are fully counted as income.

5

Result: Monthly Entitlement

The final social assistance payment results from the difference between total needs and countable resources:

Entitlement = (BLN + Rent + Health Insurance + IE*) - (Countable income + Asset depletion)

*IE = Integration allowance. Employed adults in Canton NW receive an allowance of CHF 100 per month.

Asset depletion in Canton NW:
Assets exceeding the allowances are linearly amortized over a period of 10 years and counted as monthly income:
Monthly asset depletion = Excess assets / 120
