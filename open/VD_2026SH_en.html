How is the Revenu d'insertion (RI) calculated in the Canton of Vaud? To strengthen the trust of our users, we disclose here the complete algorithm behind welfarecalc.ch. It is based on the legal foundation of the Loi sur l'action sociale (LAS VD), the associated ordinance (RRI) as well as the national SKOS guidelines 2026.

1. Asset Check (The Entry Threshold)

Allowance Individual

CHF 6'000

Plus CHF 3'000 per child or person in education.

Allowance Couples

CHF 12'000

No absolute cap for large RI households.

2. The Calculation Path (Monthly)

1

Basic Needs for Living (GBL)

The GBL is strictly based on the standardized SKOS scale 2026 for household size:

Household Size Amount per Month (2026)
1 Person CHF 1'061
2 Persons CHF 1'624
3 Persons CHF 2'017
4 Persons CHF 2'320
5 Persons CHF 2'623
Each additional person + CHF 213
2

Housing Costs & Health (Net Principle)

Crediting of housing costs up to the cantonal maximum. For health insurance, the strict net principle applies in Vaud to avoid double counting:

Recognized Rent = Min(Actual Rent, Cantonal Maximum)
Recognized Health Insurance = Max(0, KVG Premium - Individual Premium Reduction IPV)

*Note: Since the IPV in the Canton of Vaud flows directly to the insurer, our algorithm deducts the IPV directly on the expenditure side. The maximum rent guideline for 1 person is CHF 1'200 at the cantonal level.

3

Income Crediting & Earnings Allowance (Franchise)

To maintain work incentives, a statutory allowance according to RRI VD is deducted from gross or net earned income:

Creditable Income = (Net Earned Income - 20% Allowance) + Alimony + Unemployment Benefits + Other Pensions

Important: Unemployment insurance is correctly declared as ALV (Unemployment Insurance) and credited as income at 100%. A direct IPV refund is not counted again as income here.

4

Result: Monthly Entitlement

The final social welfare payment (Revenu d'insertion) is determined by covering the shortfall:

Entitlement = (GBL + Recognized Rent + Recognized Health Insurance + IZU*) - Creditable Income

*IZU = Integration Allowance (Allowance for proven participation in integration or employment measures).

Important note on legal compliance:
Unlike supplementary benefits (EL), Vaud social welfare law (LAS) does not provide for a gradual, percentage asset depletion when the entry threshold is exceeded. If liquid assets exceed the allowance, by law there is no entitlement to social welfare until the assets fall below the limit.
