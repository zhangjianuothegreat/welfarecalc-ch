Supplementary benefits (often referred to as additional benefits in the Canton of Zurich) are based on federal standards of the ELG as well as specific cantonal directives from the SVA Zurich. Here we document the mathematical core logic of our verification module in absolute accordance with legal practice as of January 1, 2026.

2. The Precise Calculation Method

1

Recognized Expenses (Needs)

The calculated total needs consist of the legally protected items:

Total Needs = Basic Needs + Recognized Rent (per capita principle) + Recognized Health Insurance Premium

Rent Maxima 2026 (Canton of Zurich applies Regions 1, 2 and 3)

The SVA Zurich assigns municipalities to Regions 1 to 3. According to the directive, a recorded Region 4 is automatically treated as Region 3. The maxima already include all ancillary costs.

Per Capita Principle (Precision Correction): If persons not included in the EL calculation share the household (e.g., flatmates, adult children), the gross rent including ancillary costs is mandatorily divided mathematically per capita (Gross rent / Total persons in the unit). The resulting share is then compared with the maximum for the corresponding household size.

Health Insurance Premium Regulation (ELG Art. 11 & SVA ZH Directive)

The statutory two-way limit (twin-lock principle) applies:

Recognized Premium = Math.min(Actual Premium, Cantonal Average Premium)

2

Countable Income (Income)

All inflows and deemed asset values are included according to modified calculation rules:

13th AHV Pension Neutralization (Statutory Requirement 2026)

According to the revised ELG, the 13th AHV pension, paid out in full for the first time in 2026, is not taken into account in the EL calculation in order to protect pensioners from losing their entitlements:

Countable AHV Pension = (Total annual pension received / 13) * 12

Earned Income

Earned income is privileged by deducting an allowance (Single persons: CHF 1,300, Married couples: CHF 1,950) and is finally recorded as income at exactly two-thirds (2/3).

Precise Asset Calculation & Deemed Consumption of Assets (Correction Factor)

If net assets exceed the allowances, a statutory portion (the deemed consumption of assets) is irrevocably added to the annual income:

Countable Income = Reduced_earned_income + Neutralized_pensions + Asset_income + Deemed_consumption_of_assets

Deemed Income from Asset Waiver (WEL 3524.01): Assets waived without legal obligation (e.g., gifts) are no longer incorrectly calculated using a rigid percentage-interest formula. Instead, the original waived amount is linearly amortized by CHF 10,000 per year, and the remaining deemed principal value is added in full to the net assets, supplemented by the official average annual savings deposit interest rate (estimated at 0.29% for the current year).
3

Final Calculation of the Annual EL

The supplementary benefit exactly closes the mathematical gap between the statutory fixed items:

Annual EL = Max(0, [Recognized Expenses] - [Countable Income])
Monthly Payout Amount = Math.round(Annual EL / 12)
Important: The Statutory Entry Threshold (Asset Threshold)
A general statutory entitlement to EL exists in the Canton of Zurich only if the adjusted net assets (excluding the value of the owner-occupied property) are strictly below CHF 100,000 for single persons or below CHF 200,000 for married couples. Exceeding this hard entry threshold leads to an immediate termination of the calculation (Entitlement = 0).
