How is social assistance calculated in the Canton of Ticino? To strengthen the trust of our users, we disclose here the complete algorithm behind welfarecalc.ch. It is based on cantonal legislation (Legge sull'assistenza sociale - LAS TI), the LAPS guidelines, and the harmonized SKOS guidelines 2026.

1. Asset Check (The Entry Threshold)

Allowance Individual / Couple

CHF 6'000 / 12'000

Plus CHF 3'000 per child. The maximum total allowance is limited to CHF 15'000 per support unit.

Asset Consumption

Divisor 10 Years

Assets exceeding the allowance are linearly counted as monthly income over 120 months.

2. The Calculation Path (Monthly)

1

Basic Living Expenses (BLE)

The BLE covers costs for food, clothing, energy, and everyday household management:

Household Size Amount per Month (2026)
1 Person CHF 1'061
2 Persons CHF 1'624
3 Persons CHF 1'974
4 Persons CHF 2'271
5 Persons CHF 2'568
Each additional person + CHF 216

*Note: For young adults in education or those living in the parental household, reduced cantonal rates apply in the Canton of Ticino (Forfait Giovani).

2

Housing Costs & Health Insurance

Recognition of actual housing and compulsory health costs up to the respective legal maximum:

Recognizable Rent (Gross) + Net Health Insurance Premium (KVG)

*Important: The maximum rent limits in Canton TI are tiered according to household size and regions/municipalities (guideline for 1 person approx. CHF 1'100). For health insurance premiums, according to cantonal practice, the ordinary cantonal premium reduction (IPV / RIPAM) is deducted directly in advance; a maximum of the cantonal average premium rate is recognized.

3

Income Assessment & Employment Deduction

An allowance is granted from the gross or net employment income to maintain the work incentive (income deduction):

Assessable Income = Other Income + (Net Employment Income × 80%)

A flat-rate employment deduction of 20% is applied to employment income. Child allowances, pensions (AHV/IV) and other LAPS benefits are counted as 100% income.

4

Result: Monthly Entitlement

The final economic assistance results from the unmet needs of the support unit:

Entitlement = (BLE + Rent + Net Health Insurance + IZA*) - (Assessable Income + Asset Consumption)

*IZA = Integration Allowance. Persons who provide an effective quid pro quo or gainful employment can receive a flat-rate integration allowance of CHF 100 per month in the Canton of Ticino.
