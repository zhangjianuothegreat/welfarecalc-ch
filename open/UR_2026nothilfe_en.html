Trust through absolute transparency. Emergency aid for individuals with a legally binding removal decision is determined in the Canton of Uri according to strict legal requirements. On this page, we disclose the complete mathematical algorithm to offer maximum traceability for those affected and advisory centers.

1. Basic Emergency Aid Parameters (2026)

Basic Daily Rate (Single Person)

CHF 8.00 / Day

Basic needs in a collective accommodation (food & clothing).

Couple Coefficient (Degression)

Factor 1.65

Two married or partnered adults receive 165% of the single rate.

Child Supplement Coefficient

Factor 0.50

Additional amount per child is 50% of the adult basic rate.

2. The Calculation Path (Step by Step)

1

Determination of the Adult Daily Rate

To avoid over-coverage, the Canton of Uri applies a degression model for multi-person households:

If Adults = 1: adultDaily = CHF 8.00
If Adults > 1: adultDaily = 8.00 + (Number of Adults - 1) × 8.00 × 0.65

Note: Due to the coefficient of 1.65 for couples, the second adult receives a reduced rate of 65% (CHF 5.20/day), which corresponds to a joint daily rate of CHF 13.20.

2

Add Child Supplements

For each child living in the same household (under 18 years), a fixed amount of 50% of the basic daily rate is added. The Canton of Uri does not apply additional progression from the 3rd child onwards.

Daily child amount = Number of Children × (8.00 × 0.50) = Number of Children × CHF 4.00
3

Monthly Extrapolation & Rounding

The flat-rate monthly extrapolation is standardized using the factor of 30.42 days. The final result is commercially rounded to the nearest whole franc in the Canton of Uri.

Monthly Entitlement = ROUND( (Total Daily Amount × 30.42) ; 0 )
Rounding Type (UR Standard) Example
Commercial rounding to 1.00 CHF 396.48 → 396.00 CHF
Round up from 0.50 CHF 396.51 → 397.00 CHF
Special features of emergency aid in the Canton of Uri:
• Priority of benefits in kind: Emergency aid only secures the constitutional minimum (Art. 12 BV). Accommodation, sanitary facilities, and basic medical care are primarily provided as benefits in kind directly by the canton (or the mandated SRK Uri).
• Day-exact accounting: Although a monthly equivalent is calculated here, the physical disbursement of cash amounts is often done weekly or daily in practice.
