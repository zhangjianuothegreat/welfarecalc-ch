How is social assistance calculated in the Canton of Glarus? Trust is built on openness. Here we disclose the complete algorithm behind welfarecalc.ch. The logic is based on the Glarus Social Assistance Act (SHG) as well as the SKOS guidelines 2026.

1. Asset Check (The Entry Threshold)

Allowance Single Person

CHF 6'000

Plus CHF 3'000 per child/person in education.

Allowance Couples

CHF 12'000

Maximum total allowance capped at CHF 15'000 per household.

2. Calculation Method (Monthly)

1

Basic Needs for Living (BNL)

The BNL 2026 in Canton GL covers costs for food, clothing, and household:

Household SizeAmount per month (2026)
1 personCHF 1'061
2 personsCHF 1'624
3 personsCHF 2'017
4 personsCHF 2'313
5 personsCHF 2'609
Each additional person+ CHF 221
2

Housing & Health Costs

Recognition of actual rental costs up to the cantonal standard rate and health insurance premiums:

Recognised Costs = Rent (up to max.) + Net KVG Premium (after IPV)

*Note: In Canton GL, the Individual Premium Reduction (IPV) is applied first. Only remaining costs are covered.

3

Income Deduction & Employment Allowance

To encourage the return to work, Canton GL grants a deduction from earned income:

Assessable Income = Net Income - Allowance (20%, max. CHF 400)

Pensions, alimony, and other social benefits are counted as income at 100%.

4

Result: Monthly Entitlement

The difference calculation determines the actual payment:

Entitlement = (Need) - (Income + Asset Consumption)

Asset consumption is 1/120 (over 10 years) of the assets exceeding the allowance, applied monthly.

Integration Allowance (IZU):
Employed individuals or those in integration measures may receive an additional allowance of CHF 100 to CHF 300 in the Canton of Glarus.
