2. The Calculation Method in Canton Zug

1

Recognized Expenses (Needs)

The mathematical sum of the legally recognized expense items:

Total Expenses = Basic Needs + Recognizable Rent + Health Insurance Premium

Recognizable Health Insurance Premiums (Priority Logic)

Our algorithm calculates the health insurance strictly according to the legal minimum principle:

Credited Premium = Math.min(Actual Premium, Cantonal Average Premium)

2

Creditable Income

The comparison of adjusted income is carried out according to clear legal requirements:

  • 13th AHV Pension (Neutralization): Since the 13th AHV pension must legally not be effective for EL from 2026, our algorithm credits pension income strictly on the basis of a maximum of 12 monthly pensions (complete legal neutralization).
  • Earned Income: Crediting of 2/3 of the amount exceeding the allowance (CHF 1'300 single persons / CHF 1'950 married couples).
  • Asset Consumption: (Adjusted net assets minus allowance) divided by the divisor 10 (for old-age pensioners) or 15 (for disability pensioners).

3

Result: Calculation of the EL Pension

If there is a coverage gap, the annual entitlement results from this:

Annual EL = Math.max(0, [Recognized Expenses] - [Creditable Income])

The monthly payout amount corresponds to the annual EL divided by 12, rounded commercially to the nearest whole franc.

Important: The legal asset threshold (entry threshold)
A general entitlement to supplementary benefits is legally excluded if the net assets (excluding the value of an owner-occupied property) exceed the following threshold values:
  • CHF 100'000 for single persons
  • CHF 200'000 for married couples
