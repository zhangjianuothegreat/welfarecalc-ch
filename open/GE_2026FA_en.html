The Canton of Geneva (GE) offers above-average family allowances compared to the federal average and is one of the few cantons that additionally grants one-time birth and adoption allowances. Our algorithm is based on official OCAS guidelines.

1. Monthly Rates & One-time Allowances (2026)

Child Allowance (1-2 children)

CHF 311 / month

Child Allowance (from 3rd child)

CHF 411 / month

Education Allowance (1-2 children)

CHF 415 / month

Education Allowance (from 3rd child)

CHF 515 / month

2. Calculation Methodology

1

Classification of Children

The algorithm checks eligibility criteria for each child:

  • 0 - 16 years: Entitled to child allowance.
  • 16 - 25 years: Entitled to education allowance (provided in education/training and income < CHF 29,400/year).
  • Disabled children: Child allowance possible up to the age of 20.
2

Calculation of Monthly Amount

Geneva uses a tiered model. The higher rate applies from the 3rd eligible child in the household:

Total = (Number of children 1&2 × Base rate) + (Number of children from 3rd × Increased rate)

*Example: For 3 children in education/training, you receive (2 × 415) + (1 × 515) = CHF 1,345 / month.

3

One-time Birth & Adoption Allowances

Unlike many other cantons, Geneva pays a one-time support for family additions:

Event Amount (GE)
Birth/Adoption (single child) CHF 2,073
Birth/Adoption (multiple births) CHF 3,073 per child
4

Calculation of Annual Entitlement

The calculator determines the cumulative amount for the calendar year:

Annual amount = (Monthly total × 12) + One-time allowances
Important for Geneva:
Allowances are processed via the cantonal compensation fund (OCAS) or the employer. The "single-parent principle" applies according to the priority order (Art. 12 FamZG).
