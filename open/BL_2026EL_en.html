2. The Official Calculation Path

1

Recognized Expenditure (Needs)

Our algorithm first aggregates all legally recognized expenditure items based on cantonal maximum limits:

Basic Needs + Rent (up to regional limit) + Health Insurance Premium (Statutory Guideline)

2

Countable Income

Offset against expenses are adjusted income and asset values:

  • Pension income & 13th AHV Reform 2026: Regular pensions are counted as 100% income. Important: As per federal decree, the newly introduced 13th AHV pension (effective 2026) is fully excluded from the EL algorithm (not added as income) to prevent a reduction in supplementary benefits.
  • Earned income: Calculated after deducting the allowance (CHF 1'000 for singles / CHF 1'500 for couples). Any remaining amount is counted at a rate of 2/3 (factor 0.666667).
  • Asset drawdown (deemed income): (Net assets + taxable property value above allowance - exemption amount) / Divisor. The divisor is 10 for old-age pensioners (AHV) and 15 for IV (disability) pensioners.

3

Result: EL Entitlement

The difference determines the annual or monthly EL amount:

Annual EL = Max(0, Recognized Expenditure - Countable Income)

The monthly benefit amount is divided by 12 and rounded commercially to the nearest Swiss franc.

Important: Asset Threshold (Entry Threshold)
A legal claim to EL in the Canton of BL only exists if the net assets (after deducting debts and the base value of owner-occupied residential property) are below CHF 100'000 for singles or CHF 200'000 for married couples.
