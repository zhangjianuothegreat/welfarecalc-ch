Trust through mathematical precision. Following our open-data philosophy, we disclose the complete calculation logic of the individual premium reduction (IPV) for the Canton of St. Gallen. Our calculation core operates strictly according to the current government decree 2026 (sGS 331.538).

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Dynamic Deductible

Variable (S-Curve)

Depending on household status and income above the basic threshold.

Asset Contribution

20.0 %

One fifth of taxable net assets increases the relevant income.

2. Official Calculation Path

1

Relevant Income (LNA)

The household's economic performance is based on net income plus asset portion minus child deductions:

LNA = [Net income] + ([Taxable assets] × 20%) - ([Number of children] × CHF 4'000)

*Note: Certain corrections such as tax-free income or capital benefits are added according to cantonal practice.

2

Regional Reference Premiums (Region 1 - 3)

The Canton of St. Gallen divides municipalities into three premium regions. The civil law residence as of January 1, 2026 is decisive:

Age Group Region 1 Region 2 Region 3
Adults (age 26 and above) CHF 6'285.60 CHF 5'879.40 CHF 5'681.40
Young adults (age 19 - 25) CHF 4'492.80 CHF 4'218.00 CHF 4'063.20
Children (age 0 - 18) CHF 1'462.80 CHF 1'351.20 CHF 1'306.80
3

S-Curve: Base Thresholds and Increase Coefficients

The reasonable co-payment (burden limit) starts at a base percentage and increases progressively for each additional franc above the base threshold:

Category (Household type) Base threshold Base rate Increase per CHF above threshold
Single without children CHF 18'700 12.16 % + 0.00020 percentage points
Married without children CHF 28'050 12.16 % + 0.00030 percentage points
Single with children CHF 18'700 + CHF 5'610 / child 10.96 % + 0.00003 percentage points
Married with children CHF 28'050 + CHF 5'610 / child 12.41 % + 0.00003 percentage points

The mathematical formula for the burden limit is:

If LNA ≤ Base threshold:
Burden limit = Base rate

If LNA > Base threshold:
Burden limit = Base rate + ((LNA - Base threshold) × Increase coefficient)
Reasonable deductible = LNA × Burden limit
4

Entitlement Calculation & Minimum Guarantee

The regular IPV entitlement is the difference between the sum of all household cantonal reference premiums and the calculated deductible:

Regular entitlement = [Total reference premiums] - [Reasonable deductible]

If the LNA is below the defined income threshold for families, the statutory minimum guarantee applies for children and young adults in education:

CategoryGuaranteed entitlement portion
Children (0-18)80% of the respective reference premium
Young adults (19-25 in education)50% of the respective reference premium
Final entitlement = Max(Regular entitlement, Statutory minimum guarantee)

3. Exclusion Criteria

Hard upper limits for entitlement exclusion (2026):
  • Income cap: If taxable income exceeds the cantonal hard limits (e.g. CHF 100,900 for single persons with 4 children or the dynamic table values of the SVA), entitlement expires completely.
  • Asset threshold: If taxable net assets exceed CHF 250,000, there is generally no entitlement to IPV.
  • De minimis threshold: If the final calculation results in an entitlement of less than CHF 100 per year, no payment will be made.
