2. Calculation Pathway

1

Relevant Income (LNA)

The LNA (Revenu déterminant) in Canton NE is determined as follows:

LNA = (Net income - Deductions) + [30% of assets*]

*Asset deduction: CHF 4,000 (Single) / CHF 8,000 (Couple) + CHF 2,000 per child.

Important: Minimum income thresholds apply: CHF 15,000 (Single) or CHF 20,000 (Couple).

2

Reference Premiums 2026

These values form the basis for capping subsidies:

CategoryAnnual Premium
AdultsCHF 7,716 / year (643 / month)
Young adults (in education)CHF 5,808 / year (484 / month)
Children (0-18)CHF 1,920 / year (160 / month)
3

Classification & Subsidy

Based on the LNA, assignment to one of 15 classes occurs:

  • Class S1: Highest subsidy (e.g., Adult CHF 611/month)
  • Class S15: Lowest subsidy (e.g., Adult CHF 41/month)
  • Children: Receive the full reference premium (CHF 160/month) in all classes S1-S15.
4

Correction Factors

Final entitlement = Subsidy × (1 - Deductible discount %)

Specific to NE: Those who choose a high deductible receive a proportionally reduced subsidy.
