Trust through transparency. Emergency aid for individuals with a removal decision in Canton Bern is calculated using a degressive household model. Here we disclose the complete algorithm for 2026—based on the EG AIG and AsylG as well as the expert information from the Coordination Office for Asylum and Refugee Affairs.

1. System Parameters & Tariffs 2026

Base Daily Rate (N ≤ 3)

CHF 10.00 / day per person

For households with 1 to 3 persons (no degression).

Degression from 4th Person

- CHF 0.50 per additional person

From 4 persons onward, the daily rate per person decreases gradually.

Boundary Tariff (N ≥ 8)

+ CHF 4.00 per extra person

Base for 7 persons CHF 56.00, then degressive increase.

2. Calculation Steps (Step by Step)

1

Determine Household Size

The calculation is based on the total number of all supported persons (adults + children) in the same accommodation unit. Children are counted the same as adults, as Canton Bern uses a flat-rate degressive system in emergency aid.

N = Number of adults + Number of children
2

Apply Degression Matrix (Daily Rate)

Depending on household size, the total daily amount is calculated according to the following public regulation:

If N ≤ 3: Daily rate = N × 10.00
If 4 ≤ N ≤ 7: Daily rate = N × (10.00 - (N - 3) × 0.50)
If N ≥ 8: Daily rate = 56.00 + (N - 7) × 4.00

Example 4 persons → 4 × CHF 9.50 = CHF 38.00/day; 5 persons → 5 × CHF 9.00 = CHF 45.00/day; 8 persons → CHF 56.00 + 4.00 = CHF 60.00/day.

3

Monthly Extrapolation & Rounding

The total daily amount is multiplied by the factor 30.42 (average number of days per month). Subsequently, commercial rounding to 0.05 CHF (5 centimes) is applied, as is customary in Swiss social welfare enforcement.

Monthly benefit = ROUND( (Total daily amount × 30.42) ; 0.05 )
Rounding Rule Example
To the nearest 0.05 CHF 247.83 → 247.85 CHF
Round down below 0.025 312.52 → 312.50 CHF
Important additional information on emergency aid in Canton Bern:
• Emergency aid is provided primarily in the form of benefits in kind (accommodation, meals); the cash benefit is kept to a minimum.
• In Canton Bern, households with more than 7 persons are subject to a special rule (base for 7 persons CHF 56.00/day, then CHF 4.00 per additional person).
• Persons without a residence permit and a legally binding removal decision are only entitled to emergency aid, not to ordinary social assistance.

3. Data Sources & Legal Basis

  • Cantonal Law: Social Welfare Act in Asylum and Refugee Matters (GSA) and Introductory Act to the Foreign Nationals and Integration Act and to the Asylum Act (EG AIG and AsylG).
  • Implementation Practice: Guidelines and expert information from the Coordination Office for Asylum and Refugee Affairs of Canton Bern (KKF).
  • Subsidiarity: Basic medical care and physical accommodation (benefits in kind) are provided directly by mandated organizations in the return centre.
