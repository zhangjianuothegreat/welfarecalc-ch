This document discloses the precise calculation algorithm used to determine statutory emergency aid (for asylum seekers, persons subject to removal, and individuals without eligible residence rights) in the Canton of Basel-Landschaft for the year 2026. This serves full transparency and verifiability for citizens, counselling services, and affected individuals.

1. Legal Basis & Parameters 2026

Emergency aid guarantees the absolute subsistence minimum under Art. 12 of the Swiss Federal Constitution. In the Canton of Basel-Landschaft, it is governed by the cantonal Social Welfare Ordinance (SHV, SGS 850.11) § 18a and the Cantonal Asylum Ordinance (kAV, SGS 850.19) § 9 .

Unlike standard social assistance, emergency aid in BL applies no degressive scaling factor (household coefficient) . Benefits are provided strictly on a per capita basis (fixed daily amount per person), as accommodation and basic medical care are provided directly as in-kind benefits by the canton or municipalities.

Regular emergency aid (§ 18a para. 5 SHV)

CHF 10.60 / day & person

For persons required to leave Switzerland, persons with a legally binding NAI (non-entry decision), etc.

Transient persons / without residence title

CHF 8.55 / day & person

According to § 18a para. 2 SHV / § 9 kAV for persons without a valid support-related residence domicile.

2. The Calculation Algorithm (Step by Step)

1

Determine the daily rate per person

First, the legal status of the persons concerned is identified in order to establish the correct daily rate:

Category / Legal status Legal basis Daily rate
Regular emergency aid recipients (persons obliged to leave / collective accommodation) SGS 850.11 § 18a para. 5 CHF 10.60
Persons without support-related domicile / transients / kAV status SGS 850.11 § 18a para. 2 / SGS 850.19 CHF 8.55
2

Household per capita calculation

The daily rate applies equally to adults and children. A linear aggregation of all persons living in the same household (or assigned accommodation unit) is performed:

Total daily rate = (Number of adults + Number of children) × Daily rate
3

Monthly projection and disbursement

In the Canton of Basel-Landschaft, emergency aid is calculated on a precise daily basis for the respective accounting month or weekly. For monthly budgeting, the exact number of calendar days (28, 29, 30, or 31 days) of that month is used. There is no fixed multiplication with a fictitious annual average (e.g., 30.42) in order to avoid cash and rounding differences.

Monthly amount = Total daily rate × Calendar days of the month

The final monthly amount is rounded commercially to the nearest 0.05 CHF.

Important note regarding in-kind benefits: The amounts of CHF 10.60 or CHF 8.55 cover exclusively the cash allowance for food, hygiene, and daily living expenses. Accommodation, heating, electricity, and emergency medical care are provided primarily as direct in-kind benefits according to § 18a para. 1 SHV and are not paid out to the persons concerned.
