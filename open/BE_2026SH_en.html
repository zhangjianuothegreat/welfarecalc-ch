Trust is built on transparency. Here we disclose the full algorithm that drives the calculations for the Canton of Bern on welfarecalc.ch. The logic is based on the Law on Social Integration and Social Welfare (SHG BE) as well as the 2026 SKOS guidelines.

2. Calculation Pathway (Monthly)

1

Basic Living Needs (BLN)

The 2026 BLN in the Canton of Bern covers food, clothing, and household items:

Household SizeAmount per Month (2026)

Each additional person+ CHF 216
2

Housing Costs (Rent)

Effective rental costs are recognized up to the cantonal maximum (Region I, II, or III):

Recognized Rent = Min(Effective Rent, Regional Maximum)

*Note: In the Canton of Bern, maximums vary. In Region II (standard), the maximum for 1 person is approx. CHF 1'100.

3

Income & Income Allowance (IA)

To maintain work incentives, an allowance is deducted from net earned income:

Countable Income = Net Income - IA (20% of earnings, max. CHF 400)

Child allowances, pensions, and IPV reimbursements are counted as regular income.

4

Result: Monthly Entitlement

The final social welfare payment results from the coverage deficit:

Entitlement = (Basic Needs + Rent + Health Insurance) - (Income + Asset Depletion)
Asset Depletion in Canton Bern:
Assets exceeding the allowance are counted as monthly income over a period of 10 years (divisor 120) until fully depleted.
