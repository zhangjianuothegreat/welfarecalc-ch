This document discloses the calculation algorithm used on welfarecalc.ch to estimate the entitlement to economic social assistance in the Canton of Valais (VS). The logic is strictly based on cantonal legislation (LES VS, LexVS 850.100) as well as the harmonized guidelines of the Swiss Conference for Social Assistance (SKOS) for the year 2026.

The Calculation Algorithm in 4 Steps

1

Entry Threshold: Asset Test

Before an entitlement to financial support arises, the liquid or realizable assets of the assistance unit (AU) must be below the cantonal allowances. Any excess assets must be fully used up.

Household Type Allowance 2026
Single person CHF 6'000
Married couples / Cohabiting couples CHF 12'000
Surcharge per minor child CHF 3'000
Maximum limit per household CHF 15'000
2

Calculation of the Social Subsistence Minimum (Needs)

The statutory total budget consists of the basic needs for living (BNL), housing costs (rent), and the costs for basic medical care (health insurance premium).

A) Standardized Basic Needs (BNL 2026)

The BNL covers food, clothing, household energy, and minimal social participation. The Canton of Valais applies the age-differentiated SKOS rates:

Household Constellation Basic Needs per Month
1-person household CHF 1'061
2-person household (e.g., couple) CHF 1'624
Additional adult in household CHF 400
Child under 11 years CHF 391
Child between 11 and 15 years CHF 699
Adolescents / Young adults (> 15 years) CHF 1'061

B) Regional Housing Costs (Rent Ceilings)

In the Canton of Valais, the maximum allowable gross rents (including ancillary costs) are strictly divided according to three geographical regions (A, B, C), based on the local price level:

Household Size Region A (e.g., Sion, Sierre) Region B (Agglomeration) Region C (Rural/Mountains)
1 Person CHF 1'200 CHF 1'100 CHF 1'000
2 Persons CHF 1'400 CHF 1'300 CHF 1'200
3 Persons CHF 1'600 CHF 1'500 CHF 1'400
4 Persons CHF 1'800 CHF 1'700 CHF 1'600
5+ Persons CHF 2'000 CHF 1'900 CHF 1'800

C) Compulsory Health Insurance (KVG)

The actual costs of the compulsory basic insurance are taken into account, but at most up to the amount of the cantonal average premium for the respective age group (minus the individual premium reduction IPV).

3

Comparison of Creditable Income

All own income of the persons living in the household is deducted from the total needs. This includes earned income, pensions (AHV/IV), alimony, and child allowances.

Creditable Income = Net earned income - Earned income allowance (income deduction) + Secondary income

Earned Income Allowance (Deduction): To maintain the incentive to work, Valais grants employed persons a deduction of 20% of net income (based on SKOS limits with cantonally defined minimum and maximum amounts depending on the workload).

Note: The cantonal individual premium reduction (IPV) is either offset directly against health insurance costs or counted as 100% income.

4

Result: Monthly Entitlement

The final economic assistance corresponds to the mathematical difference between the recognized total needs and the creditable income:

Monthly Entitlement = (BNL + Rent_effective* + Health Insurance_net) - (Creditable Income)

*Rent_effective is taken into account up to the maximum limit of the respective region (A/B/C).

Note on professional and social integration in Valais:
Persons who actively participate in integration measures or provide a service in the form of work can receive an additional Integration Allowance (IZU) of a flat rate of CHF 100 per month within the budget. For persons under 25 without basic vocational training, stricter conditions regarding the training obligation apply.
