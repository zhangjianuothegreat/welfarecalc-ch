In Switzerland, family allowances are governed by the Federal Act on Family Allowances (FamZG). Our calculator uses the specific cantonal provisions of the Canton of Nidwalden for 2026 to fully and transparently break down your entitlement.

1. Monthly Rates NW (2026)

Child Allowance

CHF 258 / month

Valid for children up to the completion of their 16th year of age.

Training Allowance

CHF 311 / month

From age 16 until the end of training (max. 25 years).

2. Calculation Method

1

Determining the Category

The algorithm checks the age, training status, and statutory income limits for each registered child:

  • 0 – 16 years: Entitlement to Child Allowance (CHF 258).
  • 16 – 25 years: Entitlement to Training Allowance (CHF 311), provided that regular training is proven and the child's earned income does not exceed the federal limit.
  • Children with disabilities: Entitlement to Child Allowance up to the completion of their 20th year of age.
2

Calculating the Monthly Amount

The Canton of Nidwalden uses a linear calculation structure. There is neither an increase starting with the third child (quantity scaling) nor age-dependent rates (age split):

(Number of children × CHF 258) + (Number in training × CHF 311)

*Note: Due to the flat rate structure, the individual rate per child remains stable regardless of the total number of children in the household.

3

One-time Allowances (Birth/Adoption)

Unlike many other cantons, the Canton of Nidwalden's legislation **does not** provide for one-time birth or adoption allowances.

Type of AllowanceAmount (NW)

4

Annual Entitlement

Since no one-time events need to be added, the annual value corresponds to the mathematically precise extrapolation of the monthly amounts:

Annual Amount = (Monthly Sum) × 12
Important information on disbursement:
The entitlement must be actively claimed with the competent compensation fund. If both parents work, the federal priority rules (Art. 12 FamZG) strictly determine who receives the allowance first. Dual payment for the same child is legally excluded.
