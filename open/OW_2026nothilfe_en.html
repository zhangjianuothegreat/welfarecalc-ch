Trust is built through absolute transparency. In the Canton of Obwalden (OW), the legal emergency aid model is strictly based on the provision of benefits in kind with minimal cash allowances. Below we disclose the exact mathematical algorithm that underlies the welfarecalc.ch calculation engine for the year 2026.

1. Legal Parameters for Canton OW (2026)

Basic Cash Allowance (Single Person)

CHF 8.00 / Day

Daily pocket money amount for a single person in a collective center.

Couple Factor / Degression Factor

Factor 1.68

Multi-person households receive a degressive factor of 168% for additional adults.

Child Allowance Factor

Factor 0.52

The additional amount per child is a constant 52% of the adult basic rate.

2. The Calculation Path (Step by Step)

1

Determine the Daily Adult Rate

The Canton of Obwalden applies a degressive system (economies of scale) as soon as more than one legally capable adult is registered in the support unit:

If adults = 1: CHF 8.00 / day
If adults > 1: 8.00 + (number of adults - 1) × 8.00 × 1.68

Note: The second adult (e.g., spouse) effectively receives 68% of the basic amount (CHF 5.44 per day).

2

Calculate the Child Supplement Amount

For each child living in the same household (under 18 years of age or in education), an amount of 52% of the basic entitlement is allocated:

Daily child amount = Number of children × (8.00 × 0.52) = Number of children × CHF 4.16 / day

Special feature: In contrast to progressive systems, the Canton of OW grants no additional bonus from the 3rd child onwards (factor remains fixed at 0.0).

3

Monthly and Annual Extrapolation

To provide statistical annual and monthly overviews, the exact daily total is extrapolated using the standardized monthly factor (30.42 days) and rounded commercially to the nearest whole franc:

Monthly entitlement (estimated) = ROUND( Daily total rate × 30.42 ; 0 )
Annual entitlement (estimated) = ROUND( Daily total rate × 30.42 × 12 ; 0 )
Special features of enforcement in Canton OW:
• Collective accommodation primacy: The calculations apply exclusively to accommodation in cantonal collective centers (accommodation and meals are covered as direct benefits in kind).
• Subsidiarity: Assets exceeding the statutory exemption limit must be fully exhausted before any claim to cash payments.
• Legal basis: Art. 82 of the Federal Asylum Act (AsylG) in close coordination with cantonal enforcement guidelines (emergency_aid_note_2026).
