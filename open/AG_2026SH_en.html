How is social welfare calculated in Aargau? To strengthen the trust of our users, we are disclosing the complete algorithm behind welfarecalc.ch. It is based on the Aargau Social Welfare and Prevention Act (SPG) as well as the SKOS guidelines 2026.

1. Asset Check (The Entry Threshold)

Allowance per person

CHF 5'000

Maximum CHF 15'000 per support unit (household).

Real Estate

Obligation to realize

Generally counted, unless owner-occupied and reasonable.

2. Calculation Method (Monthly)

1

Basic living allowance (GBL)

The GBL covers food, clothing, and household items:

Household sizeAmount per month (2026)
1 personCHF 1'061
2 personsCHF 1'624
3 personsCHF 2'017
Each additional personAccording to SKOS scale
2

Housing & Health Costs

Recognition of actual costs up to the cantonal maximum:

Gross rent + Health insurance (basic KVG coverage)

*Important: Our calculator uses the regional rent maxima of the Canton of Aargau (e.g., Aarau/Baden approx. CHF 1'100 for 1 person).

3

Income Deductions & Allowances

An allowance (income allowance EFB) is deducted from earned income to maintain work incentives:

Countable income = Net income - EFB (max. CHF 400 per person)

Additionally, child allowances and pensions are fully counted as income.

4

Result: Monthly Entitlement

The difference calculation determines the benefit payout:

Entitlement = (GBL + Rent + Health Insurance) - (Countable Income)
Note on integration:
Persons making special integration efforts may receive an additional integration allowance (IZL) of up to CHF 100 to 300 per month.
