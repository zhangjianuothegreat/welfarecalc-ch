How is social welfare calculated in the canton of Basel-Landschaft? To strengthen user trust, we disclose the mathematically precise algorithm behind welfarecalc.ch. It is based on the Social Welfare Act (SHG BL), the cantonal ordinance, and the latest SKOS guidelines 2026.

1. Asset Check (The Entry Threshold)

Before a monthly entitlement is calculated, the system checks the liquid assets of the support unit. Entitlement exists only if the allowance criterion is met:

Allowance Single Person

CHF 6'000

Plus CHF 3'000 per child or person in education/training.

Allowance Married Couples / Families

CHF 12'000

Maximum allowance per support unit in Canton BL.

2. The Calculation Pathway (Monthly)

1

Basic Living Expenses (BLE)

The BLE covers costs for food, clothing, energy, and household management according to SKOS 2026:

*Note on the SKOS scale: Our core engine v3.1 automatically accounts for the degressive reduction for child and youth allowances according to cantonal practice for larger households.

2

Housing Regions & Rent Ceilings

Actual housing costs (gross rent including ancillary costs) are recognized up to the respective regional maximum:

Recognized Rent = Min(Actual Rent, Regional Maximum)

Region B serves as the standard fallback in the system if no specific municipality is recorded.

3

Health Costs & Optimized IPV Offset

Mandatory health insurance (KVG) is covered up to the cantonal ceiling (average premium), minus the Individual Premium Reduction (IPV):

Net Health Insurance Need = Max(0, KVG Premium - Monthly IPV)

Important: Since IPV in Canton BL flows directly to the insurer, our algorithm eliminates any remaining calculation surpluses to avoid phantom income.

4

Income Accounting & Work Incentive (EFB)

An allowance is deducted from net earned income to maintain motivation to work:

Assessable Income = Net Salary - EFB (20% of salary, max. CHF 400 per month)

Other inflows such as alimony, pensions, or child allowances are added to income at 100%.

5

Result: Monthly Entitlement

Unlike pure annual estimates, our system calculates strictly monthly to track income fluctuations accurately:

Entitlement (Monthly) = Max(0, Total Need - Total Income)

Where: Total Need = BLE + Rent + Net Health Insurance + Integration Supplements (IZL)

Employment & integration supplements (IZL):
To promote professional and social integration, the law of the canton of Basel-Landschaft provides monthly supplements of CHF 100 for employed persons (subject to official classification).
