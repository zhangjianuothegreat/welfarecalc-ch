The Canton of Jura (JU) applies the Federal Act on Family Allowances (FAmA) with specific cantonal rates. Our algorithm precisely calculates your entitlement, taking into account monthly allowances as well as one-time benefits for birth or adoption.

1. Monthly rates & one-time payments (2026)

Child allowance

CHF 275 / month

From the month of birth until the end of the month in which the child turns 16.

Education allowance

CHF 325 / month

From age 16 until the end of education (maximum up to age 25).

Birth / adoption allowance

CHF 1,500 (one-time)

One-time benefit per child for residents of Canton Jura.

2. Calculation workflow

1

Categorisation of children

Each child is assigned based on age and educational status:

  • Category A (0-16 years): Child allowance (CHF 275).
  • Category B (16-25 years): Education allowance (CHF 325), provided proof of education is available.
  • Children with disabilities: Child allowance (CHF 275) until the age of 20.
2

Calculation of total monthly amount

The algorithm sums individual entitlements without additional scaling based on the number of children:

(Number of children × CHF 275) + (Number in education/training × CHF 325)

*Note: In Canton Jura, rates remain constant starting from the first child.

3

Calculation of one-time benefits

If a birth or adoption has taken place during the current year, the lump sum amount is added:

Type of allowanceAmount (JU)
Birth allowanceCHF 1,500
Adoption allowanceCHF 1,500
4

Determining the annual entitlement

The final calculation for annual planning is:

Annual amount = (Monthly sum × 12) + One-time payments
Important information for Canton Jura:
Entitlement must be claimed through the employer with the Family Compensation Fund of Canton Jura (ECAS). For self-employed persons, the process is handled directly through the cantonal fund.
