Transparency is the foundation of trust. On this page, we disclose the complete algorithm that drives calculations for the Canton of Jura on welfarecalc.ch. The logic is based on the Loi sur l'aide sociale (LAS JU) as well as the SKOS guidelines 2026.

1. Asset Test (The Eligibility Threshold)

Allowance - Single person

CHF 6,000

Plus CHF 3,000 per child or person in education/training.

Allowance - Couples

CHF 12,000

Maximum total allowance capped at CHF 15,000 per household.

2. Calculation Method (Monthly)

1

Basic Living Expenses (BLE)

The 2026 BLE in Canton Jura corresponds to the SKOS scale for living expenses:

Household Size / Person CategoryMonthly Amount (2026)
1 person (single household)CHF 1,061
2 persons (couple/cohabiting)CHF 1,624
Additional adult (3rd+ adult)+ CHF 400
Child (< 11 years)CHF 391
Child (11-15 years)CHF 699
2

Housing Costs (Regional Maxima)

Canton Jura distinguishes between urban (Urbain) and rural (Rural) zones:

Recognised rent = Min(Actual rent, Zone maximum)

*Example (1 person): Urban CHF 1,200 / Rural CHF 1,000. Our algorithm automatically recognises the cheaper or official zone assignment.

3

Income & Employment Allowance (EFA)

To promote professional integration, Canton Jura grants a deduction from net earned income:

Assessable income = Net income - EFA (20% flat-rate deduction)

Integration allowances (IZL) of CHF 100 may be granted upon active participation.

4

Result: Monthly Entitlement

The monthly benefit amount is calculated by covering the deficit:

Entitlement = (BLE + Rent + Health insurance premium) - (Income + Asset consumption)

*Health insurance premiums are recognised up to the cantonal standard amount (approx. CHF 350-500 depending on situation).

Canton Jura specific feature:
The calculation takes into account specific cantonal allowances for persons in education (Personnes en formation), who may receive the same basic living expense rate as persons aged 15 and over.
