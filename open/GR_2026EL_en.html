Transparency is the cornerstone of our work at welfarecalc.ch . The canton of Graubünden applies the Federal Supplementary Benefits Act (ELG) with specific cantonal parameters for rent and health insurance premiums. Here you'll learn how our system calculates your entitlement.

1. Annual Basic Needs (Federal Minimum Rate 2026)

Single persons

CHF 20,670

Annual amount for general living expenses.

Married couples

CHF 31,005

Combined basic allowance for couples in the same household.

2. Detailed Calculation Steps

1

Step 1: Recognized Expenses (Needs)

We add all legally recognized cost components:

Basic needs + Gross rent (up to cantonal limit) + Health insurance flat-rate premium
Maximum rent limits Graubünden 2026 (Region 2*) Amount per year
1-person household CHF 18,300 (CHF 1,525/month)
2-person household CHF 21,720 (CHF 1,810/month)
3-person household CHF 23,760 (CHF 1,980/month)

*Note: In rural areas of Graubünden (Region 3), lower limits apply (e.g., CHF 1,390 for singles).

2

Step 2: Countable Income

The following own resources are offset against expenses:

  • AHV/IV Pensions: 12 monthly pensions are counted. The 13th AHV pension remains neutral according to federal resolution and does not reduce your EL entitlement.
  • Asset depletion: (Net assets - allowance) / 15 (for AHV pensioners) or 1/15 (for IV).
  • Earned income: Counting after deduction of statutory flat-rate allowance and 1/3 reduction.
Asset allowances Amount
Single persons CHF 30,000
Married couples CHF 50,000
Owner-occupied residential property CHF 112,500
3

Step 3: Calculation of EL amount

The supplementary benefit covers the deficit:

Annual EL = [Sum of recognized expenses] - [Sum of countable income]
Asset threshold (Eligibility threshold)
Entitlement in the canton of Graubünden exists only if taxable net assets are below CHF 100,000 (single persons) or CHF 200,000 (married couples). The value of an owner-occupied property is excluded from this threshold.
