Transparency builds trust. In the Canton of Jura (JU), emergency aid for persons with a legally binding removal decision is calculated using specific cantonal rates. Here we disclose the full algorithm used in our calculator.

1. Core Parameters (JU 2026)

Basic Daily Rate (Individual)

CHF 8.00 / day

Applies to individuals in collective accommodation.

Couple Factor (Degression)

Factor 1.65

Two adults receive 165% of the individual rate.

Child Supplement Factor

Factor 0.50

Additional coverage per child (50% of the basic rate).

Multi-Child Bonus (from 3rd child)

Factor 0.10

Additional bonus per child from the third child onward.

2. Calculation Method (Step by Step)

1

Adult Component

First, the daily amount for adults is determined. Jura uses a degressive scale for couples:

Single person = CHF 8.00
Couple (2 adults) = 8.00 + (8.00 × 0.65) = CHF 13.20
2

Child Component & Bonus

The child rate is added per child. From the third child onward, an additional bonus factor of 0.10 applies:

Child base = Number of children × (8.00 × 0.50)
Bonus (if children ≥ 3) = (Number of children - 2) × (8.00 × 0.10)
3

Monthly Extrapolation & Rounding

The total daily rate is extrapolated to an average month (30.42 days) and rounded to the nearest Swiss franc (commercial rounding).

Monthly amount = ROUND( (Daily total × 30.42) ; 0 )
Important note for JU:
• The rates are based on accommodation in cantonal collective facilities.
• Emergency aid is primarily provided as benefits in kind.
• The cash allowance is intended exclusively to cover the most basic personal needs.
