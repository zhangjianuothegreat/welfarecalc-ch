Transparency creates trust. Supplementary benefits (EL) to AHV/IV follow clear legal frameworks. Here we document openly and transparently how our calculator determines your entitlement in the canton of Thurgau based on federal law (ELG) and cantonal regulations.

Combined statutory basic needs for both spouses.

2. The Calculation Path in Detail

1

Recognized Expenditure (Needs)

First, the algorithm calculates the sum of the legally recognized expenditure items:

Recognized Expenditure = Basic Needs + Recognized Rent + Health Insurance Premiums

A) Rent Maxima 2026 (Canton TG)

The residential region is determined automatically via the postal code (PLZ) (Region 2: Cities/Agglomeration like Frauenfeld, Arbon, Kreuzlingen; Region 3: Rural municipalities).

B) Health Insurance Premiums (Legal Cap)

The algorithm compares your actual paid premium with the cantonal average premium. The principle "minimum of actual premium and cantonal flat rate" applies:

Recognized Premium = Minimum(Actual Premium, Cantonal Flat Rate)
Age Group (Cantonal Flat Rates p.a.) Amount
Adults (from 26 years) CHF 6,636

2

Creditable Income

Then, all creditable income is determined and compared:

  • Pensions & Income: Regular pensions (AHV/IV) are fully credited. Important: The 13th AHV pension is legally neutralized and is not included in the income!
  • Earned Income: After deducting the statutory allowance (CHF 1,300 for single persons / CHF 1,950 for couples), two-thirds (2/3) is credited.
  • Asset Consumption: If net assets exceed the allowance, a statutory portion is credited as income.

Asset Allowances (For crediting consumption)

Category Allowance
Single Persons CHF 30,000
Married Couples CHF 50,000
Additional for owner-occupied properties CHF 112,500

Algorithm for Asset Consumption (p.a.)

Allowance = Couple ? 50,000 : 30,000;
Divisor = (Recipient == AHV old-age pension) ? 10 : 15;
Creditable_Consumption = Max(0, (Net Assets - Allowance) / Divisor);
3

Determination of EL Entitlement

The annual EL pension closes the financial gap between the two totals:

Annual EL Benefit = Max(0, [Recognized Expenditure] - [Creditable Income])

The monthly payout corresponds to the Annual Benefit divided by 12.

Important: The statutory asset threshold (entry hurdle)
A legal entitlement to supplementary benefits exists in the canton of Thurgau (as throughout Switzerland) only if net assets are below CHF 100,000 (for single persons) or below CHF 200,000 (for married couples). The value of an owner-occupied property is not counted towards this entry threshold.
