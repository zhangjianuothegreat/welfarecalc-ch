In Switzerland, family allowances are regulated by the federal law (FamZG). Our calculator uses the specific legal provisions of the Canton of Ticino for the year 2026 to determine your entitlement completely transparently and precisely.

1. Monthly Rates in Ticino (2026)

Child Allowance

CHF 215 / Month

For children from the month of birth until the completion of the 16th year of age.

Education Allowance

CHF 268 / Month

From the month following the 16th birthday until the end of education (max. 25).

2. The Calculation Path

1

Determination of the Entitlement Category

The mathematical core of our calculator checks the age and the current educational status of each child:

  • 0 - 16 years: Legal entitlement to child allowance (CHF 215).
  • 16 - 25 years: Legal entitlement to education allowance (CHF 268), provided recognized education or studies are proven.
  • Disabled children: Entitlement to child allowance until the completion of the 20th year of age.
2

Calculation of the Monthly Amount

The Canton of Ticino applies a linear calculation model without gradations or increases based on the number of children:

Monthly total = (Number of children × CHF 215) + (Number in education × CHF 268)

*Note: In Ticino, there are no increases for large families (e.g., from the 3rd child onwards), as is practiced in some other Swiss cantons.

3

One-time Allowances (Birth / Adoption)

The Canton of Ticino (Istituto delle assicurazioni sociali TI) grants no one-time birth or adoption allowances according to cantonal law (Laf/RegLaf).

Allowance Type Amount (TI)
Birth Allowance CHF 0
Adoption Allowance CHF 0
4

Determination of the Annual Entitlement

The monthly determined values are extrapolated to the calendar year. Payment is usually made directly via the employer:

Annual Entitlement = (Monthly Total Entitlement) × 12
Legal Minimum Income Threshold (FamZG):
To claim full entitlement as an employee, a minimum OASI-liable income of at least CHF 630 per month or CHF 7,560 per year must be earned. If the income is below this, entitlement as a non-employed person can be examined.
