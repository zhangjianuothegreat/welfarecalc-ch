How is social assistance calculated in the city canton? To ensure maximum transparency, we disclose the welfarecalc.ch algorithm. The logic is based on the social assistance legislation of the Canton of Basel-Stadt (SG 890.100) as well as the SKOS guidelines 2026.

1. Asset Check (Eligibility Test)

Allowance Single Person

CHF 8'000

For married couples, an amount of CHF 16'000 applies.

Household Maximum

CHF 20'000

Including child allowances (CHF 4'000 per child).

2. Calculation Method (Monthly)

1

Basic Living Needs (BLN)

The BLN covers costs for food, hygiene, and household. In 2026, Basel-Stadt applies the following rates:

Person CategoryAmount per month (2026)
1 person (adult)CHF 1'061
2 persons (couple)CHF 1'624
Young adults in education (18-25 yrs)CHF 812*
Child (under 11 years)CHF 391
Child (11 to 15 years)CHF 699

*Reduced rate for young adults in education according to BS practice.

2

Housing Costs (Rent Ceilings)

Calculation of actual net rent up to the cantonal maximum. Basel-Stadt uses a unified system:

Recognized Rent = Min(Actual Rent, BS Maximum)
Household sizeMax. Rent (Net)
1 personCHF 880
2 personsCHF 1'210
3 personsCHF 1'390
4 personsCHF 1'650
3

Health Insurance & Premium Reduction (IPV)

Health insurance premiums (KVG) are considered in the budget, with the cantonal premium reduction directly offset:

Net Health Insurance Need = Actual Premium - Individual Premium Reduction (IPV)
4

Income Calculation & Employment Allowance

Employed individuals receive an allowance (EFB) to reward work incentives:

Assessable Income = Net Income - 20% EFB (max. CHF 400/month)

Additionally, an integration allowance (IZL) of CHF 100 may be granted if earned income is generated.

5

Result: Monthly Entitlement

Coverage of needs is calculated as follows:

Entitlement = (BLN + Rent + Health Net + IZL) - (Assessable Income + Asset Depletion)
Note on Asset Depletion:
Assets above the allowances are linearly considered as income over a period of 7 years (84 months) in the Canton of Basel-Stadt.
