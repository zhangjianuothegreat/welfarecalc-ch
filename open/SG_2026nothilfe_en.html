Trust through transparency. Emergency aid for persons required to leave Switzerland is determined in the Canton of St. Gallen according to precise legal requirements. Here we disclose the adjusted, official algorithm that forms the basis of our calculation engine.

1. Basic Emergency Aid Parameters (SG 2026)

Basic Daily Rate (Single person)

CHF 10.00 / day

Basic needs for a single person in a collective accommodation facility.

Couple Lump Sum (Degressive)

Factor 1.72 (CHF 17.20 / day)

Two adults in the same household receive 172% of the single rate together.

Child Supplement

Factor 0.55 (CHF 5.50 / day)

Additional amount per child (55% of the adult basic rate).

2. The Calculation Path (Step by Step)

1

Determining the Adult Daily Rate

The Canton of St. Gallen uses a degressive model to account for household savings:

If adults = 1: CHF 10.00 per day
If adults = 2: CHF 17.20 per day (Basic rate × 1.72)
If adults > 2: CHF 17.20 + (number of adults - 2) × CHF 7.20 per day

Note: According to cantonal practice, for each additional adult within the same support unit, the degressive rate of CHF 7.20 is added.

2

Add Child Supplement Amount

For each child (< 18 years) living in the household, a lump sum amount is added, which corresponds exactly to 55% of the adult basic rate:

Total Child Daily Amount = Number of children × (10.00 × 0.55) = Number of children × CHF 5.50 / day
3

Monthly Extrapolation & Administrative Rounding

The total daily entitlement is extrapolated based on the statistical monthly factor (30.42 days). Commercial rounding is performed in the Canton of SG as standard to the nearest 10 Rappen (0.10 CHF).

Monthly entitlement = ROUND( (Total daily amount × 30.42) ; 0.10 )
Important administrative limitations in Canton SG:
• In-kind benefits priority: Emergency aid in the Canton of St. Gallen is primarily provided as in-kind benefits (accommodation in collective structures, direct food distribution or vouchers). The cash amount calculated here only applies if in-kind benefits cannot be fully provided.
• No long-term entitlement: Emergency aid only secures day-to-day survival (Art. 12 BV). Monthly or annual advance payments are legally excluded. The monthly calculation serves purely statistical purposes.
