Trust through transparency. Emergency aid for persons subject to a departure decision is calculated in the Canton of Aargau according to clear legal criteria. Here we disclose the complete algorithm that underpins our calculator – based on official guidelines from cantonal authorities.

1. Basic Emergency Aid Parameters (2026)

Basic daily rate (single person)

CHF 8.00 / day

Collective accommodation, incl. meals & basic needs.

Couple flat rate

CHF 13.60 / day

For two adults in the same facility (degression).

Child supplement

CHF 4.15 / day per child

Up to the age of 18, regardless of the number.

2. Calculation Method (Step by Step)

1

Determine the adult daily rate

First, the daily basic amount is determined based on the number of adults:

Single person: CHF 8.00 per day
Married couple / partnership (2 adults): CHF 13.60 per day (single + 70% supplement)

If there are more than 2 adults per support unit, each additional adult is charged at the single rate (according to Aargau practice).

2

Add child supplements

For each child (< 18 years), a fixed amount of CHF 4.15 per day is added. The supplement covers additional needs for food, hygiene, and clothing.

Daily total amount = Adult daily rate + (Number of children × CHF 4.15)
3

Monthly extrapolation & rounding

Emergency aid is calculated based on 30.42 days (average month). The final amount is rounded commercially to 5 Rappen (0.05 CHF), as is customary in cantonal implementation.

Monthly entitlement = ROUND( (Daily total amount × 30.42) ; 0.05 )
Rounding rule Example
Round to nearest 0.05 247.83 → 247.85 CHF
Round down below 0.025 312.52 → 312.50 CHF
Important additional information on emergency aid:
• Emergency aid is primarily provided in kind (accommodation, meals); the cash allowance is kept to a minimum.
• Assets exceeding the allowance (CHF 5,000 per person / max. CHF 15,000 per household) must be used first.
• Persons without a residence permit who are subject to a legally binding departure decision are only entitled to emergency aid, not to ordinary social welfare.
