Unlike cantons with rigid income brackets, the Canton of Vaud uses a highly developed, socially equitable RDU model (Revenu déterminant unifié). The canton's goal is to cover the burden of compulsory health insurance (KVG) to approx. 10% of the relevant income.

Cantonal Parameters for 2026

Maximum Monthly Subsidy (Adults)

CHF 331.00 / Month

Minimum Monthly Subsidy (Adults)

CHF 30.00 / Month

Asset Adjustment Rate (RDU)

6.7% (1/15th of net assets)

Deductible Ceiling (Burden)

Step-by-Step Calculation Logic

1

Determination of the Adjusted RDU

The cantonal RDU is based on the definitive tax assessment, adjusted for specific Vaud legislation (exclusion of private debts and additions):

RDU = Taxable net income
+ Deductions for payments into pillar 3a
+ Health insurance premiums deducted for tax purposes
+ [Available net assets - allowance] × 6.7%
- Lump-sum deductions (e.g., married couple deduction: CHF 4'400, child deduction: CHF 1'300)

*Note: In the Canton of Vaud, private debts or mortgages are NOT considered as a reduction in the IPV asset test!

2

Calculation of the Ordinary Subsidy (Subside ordinaire)

The Canton of Vaud uses a logarithmically smoothed, progressive curve function to calculate the ordinary entitlement. For standard UER households, the following mathematical parameters apply:

If RDU ≤ CHF 16'000 (Limit C6):
  Subside_Ordinaire = Maximum reference premium of the household

If CHF 16'000 < RDU ≤ CHF 40'000 (Limit A6):
  Curve Factor = P6 (2.3) & Q6 (0.8)
  Subside_Ordinaire = Calculated via the sliding cantonal decompression curve formula.

Application of legal minimum limits:
  The monthly ordinary subsidy per adult is set at a minimum of CHF 30.00 and capped at a maximum of CHF 331.00.
3

The Safety Net: Specific Subsidy (Subside spécifique)

This is the most important correction factor that standard calculators miss. If, after deducting the ordinary subsidy, the remaining premium burden on the household is unreasonably high, the 10% safety net applies:

Allowable household premium burden = Total reference premiums of the household
Remaining burden = Allowable household premium burden - Subside_Ordinaire_Year

Cantonal reasonableness threshold = RDU × 10%

If remaining burden > Reasonableness threshold:
  Subside_Spécifique = Remaining burden - Reasonableness threshold
Otherwise:
  Subside_Spécifique = 0
4

Final Total Entitlement & Capping

The definitive payout amount results from the addition of both subsidy components and is limited by the actual, effective premium of the insured person:

Entitlement_Absolute = Subside_Ordinaire + Subside_Spécifique
Final_Entitlement = Min(Entitlement_Absolute, Effective_Health_Insurance_Premium_Household)

Amounts under CHF 100.00 per year are not paid out for efficiency reasons (de minimis threshold).

Asset Exclusion Limit (Upper Limit):
If the adjusted net assets (without deduction of debts) exceed CHF 100'000 for single persons or CHF 160'000 for married couples, the legal entitlement to the premium reduction in the Canton of Vaud expires completely – regardless of how low the RDU income may be.
