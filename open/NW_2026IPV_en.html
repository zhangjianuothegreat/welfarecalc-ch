Trust through transparency. Here we disclose the mathematical foundation used by our calculator to determine your individual premium reduction (IPV / Prämienverbilligung) in the canton of Nidwalden. This algorithm strictly follows the official legal provisions of Canton NW and the Nidwalden Compensation Office (AKNW).

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Statutory Deductible

10.0 %

Share of the relevant income considered reasonable for health insurance costs (§ 1 para. 1).

Wealth Assessment Rate

20.0 %

Share of taxable net assets added as imputed income (§ 1 para. 2).

2. Calculation Pathway

1

Relevant Income (LNA)

First, the economic capacity is determined based on tax data (Relevant tax period: 2024):

LNA = Max(0, [Net income] + [20% of taxable net assets] + [Additional components] - [Child deduction])

* Child deduction: CHF 5,000 per child or young adult in education/training.

2

Official Standard Premiums 2026

The canton of NW sets the annual reference values for insurance costs (other insured persons):

Category (Civil status / Age)Standard Premium 2026
Adults (aged 26 and over)CHF 5,400 / year (CHF 450 / month)
Young adults in education (19-25 years)CHF 3,312 / year (CHF 276 / month)
Children (0-18 years)CHF 1,260 / year (CHF 105 / month)
3

Calculation of Statutory Entitlement

The 10% deductible of the LNA is subtracted from the total of all standard premiums of household members:

Calculated entitlement = Total standard premiums - (LNA × 10%)

If the relevant income exceeds the economic affordability threshold, the calculated entitlement mathematically decreases to CHF 0.

4

Statutory Limits & Exclusions

The final disbursement amount is subject to the following cantonal adjustments:

  • De minimis threshold: Amounts below CHF 100.00 per year are not paid out according to the law (§ 5 para. 1).
  • Actual premium cap: The IPV contribution is strictly limited upward by the premium actually paid to the health insurance company.
Special feature of Canton Nidwalden:
The 20% wealth assessment rate is exceptionally high compared to other Swiss cantons. This means that persons with moderate income but substantial savings (assets) may quickly exceed the IPV eligibility threshold because one-fifth of their net wealth is added to their income.
