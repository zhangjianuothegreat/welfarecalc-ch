Trust is built through transparency. Here we disclose the complete algorithm that controls the calculations for the Canton of Appenzell Innerrhoden on welfarecalc.ch. The logic is based on the cantonal social assistance ordinance and the SKOS guidelines 2026.

1. Asset Check (The Entry Threshold)

Allowance for Single Person

CHF 6,000

Plus CHF 2,000 per child/person in training.

Allowance for Married Couples

CHF 12,000

Maximum total allowance capped at CHF 20,000 per household.

2. The Calculation Path (Monthly)

1

Basic Needs for Living (GBL)

The GBL 2026 in the Canton of AI follows the SKOS inflation adjustment:

Household Size Amount per Month (2026)
1 person CHF 1,061
2 persons CHF 1,624
3 persons CHF 1,974
4 persons CHF 2,271
5 persons CHF 2,568
Each additional person + CHF 216
2

Housing Costs (Rent)

Accounting of effective rental costs incl. ancillary costs up to the cantonal maximum:

Recognised Rent = Min(Effective Rent, Regional Maximum)

*Note: In the Canton of AI, the reference values for a single-person household are generally around CHF 950 (gross).

3

Income & Earned Income Allowance (EFB)

To maintain the work incentive, an allowance is deducted from net earned income:

Accountable Income = Net Income - EFB (20% of salary, max. CHF 400)

Other income (pensions, IV/AI reimbursement, maintenance payments) is accounted at 100%.

4

Result: Monthly Entitlement

The final social assistance payment results from the shortfall in needs:

Entitlement = (GBL + Rent + Health Insurance + SIL*) - (Income + Asset Consumption)

*SIL = Situation-related benefits (e.g. dentist, employment-related expenses).

Asset Consumption in the Canton of AI:
Assets exceeding the allowance must be consumed before receiving social assistance. In the case of real estate, a mortgage-backed security for repayment may be required.
