Trust is built through absolute transparency. We disclose here the complete algorithm that drives the calculations for the Canton of Obwalden (OW) on welfarecalc.ch. The logic fully complies with the legal provisions of the Obwalden Social Assistance Act (SHG OW) and the Switzerland-wide harmonized SKOS guidelines 2026.

1. Asset Check (The Initial Threshold)

Allowance for Single Person

CHF 6'000

Increased according to SKOS revision effective 01.01.2026 (previously CHF 4'000).

Allowance for Couples / Families

CHF 12'000

Plus CHF 3'000 per child. Total maximum allowance capped at CHF 15'000 per support unit.

2. The Calculation Path (Monthly)

1

Basic Living Allowance (BLA)

The BLA covers costs for food, clothing, energy, and household management. It is strictly calculated according to the official SKOS support unit (number of persons in the same household):

Household SizeAmount per month (SKOS 2026)
1 personCHF 1'061
2 personsCHF 1'624
3 personsCHF 1'954
4 personsCHF 2'240
5 personsCHF 2'526
Each additional person+ CHF 201
2

Housing Costs (Rent Limits Canton OW)

Allocation of actual rental costs (gross rent including ancillary costs and heating) up to the officially recognized cantonal maximum:

Household SizeMax. recognized gross rent (Canton OW)
1 personCHF 1'100
2 personsCHF 1'400
3 personsCHF 1'650
4 personsCHF 1'850
5+ personsCHF 2'000
Recognized rent = Min(Actual rent, Cantonal maximum)
3

Health Costs & IPV Premium Reduction

Costs for compulsory health insurance (KVG) are included in the budget. According to the official practice of Canton OW, the Individual Premium Reduction (IPV) is directly applied first:

Recognized premium need = Max(0, KVG premium - Monthly IPV)

*Note: Either the cantonal reference premium or the actual premium (if lower) is used.

4

Income & Employment Allowance (EFB)

To ensure incentives to resume gainful employment, employed persons receive a lump-sum allowance on their net income. This is calculated as follows:

Assessable income = Net income - EFB (Min(400, Max(200, Net income × 0.2)))

Other income (e.g., child allowances, all types of pensions, alimony) and asset consumption (if assets exceed allowances) are fully counted as income.

5

Result: Monthly Entitlement

The final legal social assistance payment in the Canton of Obwalden results from the mathematical coverage deficit:

Entitlement = (BLA + Recognized rent + Health insurance need + IE*) - (Assessable income)

*IE = Integration allowance for verifiable integration efforts or gainful employment (usually CHF 100 to CHF 300 per month).

Asset Depletion in Canton Obwalden (OW):
Liquid assets and wealth exceeding the cantonal allowances during initial assessment must be depleted before receiving economic social assistance. Regarding real estate, cantonal provisions for mortgage collateralization and reimbursement obligations apply.
