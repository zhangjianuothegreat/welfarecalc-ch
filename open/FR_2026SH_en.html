Transparency is the cornerstone of trust. On this page, we document the complete algorithm for the Canton of Fribourg as used on welfarecalc.ch. Our calculations are based on the Social Assistance Act (LAS) of the Canton of Fribourg as well as the 2026 SKOS guidelines.

1. Asset Check (Eligibility Threshold)

Allowance for Single Person

CHF 6'000

Basic allowance for single individuals in Canton FR.

Allowance for Couples

CHF 12'000

Plus CHF 3'000 per child (max. CHF 15'000 total allowance).

2. Monthly Calculation Process

1

Basic Living Expenses (GBL)

In the Canton of Fribourg, the GBL covers general living costs (food, clothing, hygiene):

Household Size Amount per month (2026)
1 person CHF 1'061
2 persons CHF 1'624
3 persons CHF 2'017*
Additional adult + CHF 400
Additional child Staggered by age

*Based on the standardized SKOS scale for 2026.

2

Housing Costs & Regions

Fribourg applies regional rent caps (Region A: Urban centers, B: Agglomeration, C: Rural):

Recognized Rent = Min(Actual Rent, Regional Maximum)

Example Region A (Urban): guideline approx. CHF 1'200 for 1 person. Our calculator automatically assigns your postcode to the correct region.

3

Income Calculation & Deductions

To encourage professional integration, an allowance is deducted from earned income:

Countable Income = Net Income - Earned Income Deduction (20%)

Social security benefits (pensions, unemployment insurance) and family allowances are counted as 100% income.

4

Result: Monthly Entitlement

The difference between needs and income determines the benefit amount:

Entitlement = (GBL + Rent + Health Insurance + IEA*) - (Income + Asset Depletion)

*IEA = Integration Incentive Allowance (up to CHF 100-300 when working or participating in integration).

Canton of Fribourg Special Feature:
In Canton FR, health insurance premiums are generally covered only up to the amount of the cantonal average premium (minus IPV). Our algorithm calculates this deduction dynamically based on current premium reduction rates.
