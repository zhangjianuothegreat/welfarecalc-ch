Basel-Stadt relies on a transparent model to secure the minimum subsistence level. Here we disclose the official algorithm for 2026. Compared to other cantons, Basel-Stadt stands out with a higher daily allowance to account for the urban standard of living.

1. Core Parameters (BS 2026)

Basic Daily Rate (Single Person)

CHF 11.00 / day

Highest rate in Switzerland, including pocket money for personal needs.

Couple Factor (Degression)

Factor 1.80

Shared households receive 180% of the basic rate (CHF 19.80 for 2 persons).

Child Supplement Factor

Factor 0.60

Additional amount per child: 60% of basic rate (CHF 6.60 / day).

2. Calculation Steps

1

Determine Adult Needs

Basel-Stadt uses a degressive model for multi-person households:

Single person: CHF 11.00 / day
Couple/Multi-person: 11.00 + (Adults - 1) × 11.00 × 0.80

Note: From the second adult onwards, the need is weighted at 80%.

2

Add Child Allowance

Each child receives a fixed factor of 0.60 of the basic rate, regardless of number:

Child total = Number of children × (11.00 × 0.60) = Number × CHF 6.60 / day
3

Monthly Calculation & Rounding

The daily amount is multiplied by the Swiss standard factor of 30.42. The final amount is rounded to the nearest Swiss franc in Basel-Stadt.

Monthly entitlement = ROUND( (Daily Total × 30.42) ; 0 )
Important notes for Basel-Stadt:
• Rates apply when accommodated in cantonal reception centers.
• Health insurance premiums and emergency medical care are covered additionally.
• In case of unexcused absence, the cash allowance may be reduced.
