3. The Calculation Path in Detail

1

Recognized Expenditure (Needs Assessment)

First, the system adds up all legally recognized and canton-specific expenditure items:

Basic Needs + Recognized Rent (incl. ancillary costs) + Health Insurance Premium (+ possibly wheelchair supplement)

Rent Maxima 2026 in the Canton of Vaud (incl. ancillary costs)

The rent limits depend on the official postal code regions of the canton. The statutory flat rates already include all ancillary costs:

Vaud Special Rule: For certified need for wheelchair-accessible housing, the annual rent maximum automatically increases by a flat rate of CHF 6'900.

Health Insurance Premium Regulation

Primarily, the effectively paid premium is credited (up to a maximum of CHF 30'000 p.a.). If no value is available or it is negative, the cantonal average premiums apply (e.g., Region 1: CHF 8'388, Region 2: CHF 7'860, Region 3: CHF 7'284 annually per adult).

2

Creditable Income & Asset Consumption

The adjusted economic income is compared to the expenditure:

  • AHV/IV Pensions (Strict 13th Pension Neutralization): Since the introduction of the 13th AHV pension in 2026, the algorithm mathematically filters out this component cleanly. The rule is: Adjusted Pension = (Total AHV Pension / 13) * 12. Other, non-AHV income remains 100% uneducted.
  • Employment Income: Adjusted for the statutory allowance (CHF 1'300 for single persons / CHF 1'950 for couples), then 2/3 of the remaining amount is standardly credited.
  • Rental Income & Alimony: Direct crediting according to tax values.
  • Asset Consumption (Cantonal Divisors): The cantonal home ownership deduction (CHF 112'500 for single persons, CHF 187'500 for married couples for owner-occupied residential property) and the statutory allowance are deducted from net assets. The remainder is divided by the specific divisor. Fictitiously transferred assets (waived assets) are included with a return of 0.29%.

3

Result: EL Calculation

The supplementary benefit closes the exact gap between the two figures:

Annual EL Benefit = Max(0, [Recognized Expenditure] - [Creditable Income])

The monthly payout amount corresponds to the annual EL divided by 12, rounded commercially to the nearest whole franc.

Important: The statutory entry threshold (Asset threshold)
A legal entitlement to supplementary benefits is absolutely excluded if the taxable net assets (before deduction of allowances, but excluding the value of an owner-occupied property) exceed the following thresholds:
  • Single Persons: CHF 100'000
  • Married Couples: CHF 200'000
