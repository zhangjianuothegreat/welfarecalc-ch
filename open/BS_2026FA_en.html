The Canton of Basel-Stadt grants family allowances significantly above the Swiss federal minimum. Our algorithm is based on the cantonal implementation law for the Federal Act on Family Allowances (EG FamZG BS) and the current rates for 2026.

1. Monthly Rates in BS (2026)

Child Allowance

CHF 275 / month

From the month of birth until the end of the month in which the child turns 16.

Training Allowance

CHF 325 / month

From age 16 until completion of education/training (maximum until age 25).

2. Algorithmic Calculation Path

1

Categorization & Age Verification

The system checks for each child the status:

  • Status A (Child): Age < 16 years.
  • Status B (Training): Age 16–25 years + proof of enrollment in education/training.
  • Children with disabilities: Entitled to child allowance (CHF 275) until age 20 (under certain conditions).
2

Monthly Benefit Calculation

The calculation is linear without degression or progression:

Monthly amount = (Number of Status A × CHF 275) + (Number of Status B × CHF 325)

*Note: Unlike cantons such as Geneva or Vaud, Basel-Stadt does not increase the amount starting with the 3rd child.

3

Verification of One-Time Benefits

An important difference in the BS algorithm compared to other cantons:

Allowance Type Amount (BS) Remark
Birth allowance CHF 0 No legal entitlement in BS
Adoption allowance CHF 0 No legal entitlement in BS
4

Annual Entitlement (Projection)

For budget planning, our tool calculates the annual value:

Total annual amount = Monthly amount × 12
Important for cross-border commuters:
Employees who work in Basel-Stadt but reside abroad (e.g., Germany or France) are also entitled to differential payments according to EU/EFTA regulations. Our algorithm takes this into account when entering the place of residence.
