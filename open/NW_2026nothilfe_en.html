Trust through transparency. Emergency aid for individuals subject to a removal decision is calculated in the Canton of Nidwalden according to clear, statutory criteria. Here we disclose the full algorithm underlying our calculator – based on official cantonal guidelines.

2. Calculation Method (Step by Step)

1

Determine adult daily rate (degression)

First, the daily basic amount is determined based on the number of adults. The Canton of Nidwalden applies a degressive model to reflect household savings:

Single person: CHF 8.00 per day
Couple (2 adults): CHF 8.00 + 8.00 × 1.68 = CHF 13.44 per day
For more than 2 adults: Each additional person receives an extra CHF 8.00 × 1.68 (degressive increase).

Practice according to guidelines of Canton Nidwalden: synergy effects are fairly accounted for.

2

Child allowances & large-family bonus

For each child (< 18 years), an allowance of 52% of the adult basic rate (CHF 8.00) is added. To support larger families, the Canton of Nidwalden grants an additional bonus of 10% of the basic rate for each child beyond the second.

Basic child total = Number of children × (CHF 8.00 × 0.52)
Bonus from 3rd child onward: (Number of children - 2) × (CHF 8.00 × 0.10)
Daily total = Adult daily rate + Basic child total + Bonus
3

Monthly extrapolation & rounding

Emergency aid is calculated based on 30.42 days (average month). The final amount is rounded commercially to 5 Rappen (CHF 0.05), as standard in cantonal implementation.

Monthly entitlement = ROUND( (Daily total × 30.42) ; 0.05 )

Important supplementary information on emergency aid in Canton Nidwalden:
• Emergency aid is primarily provided in kind (accommodation, meals); the cash component is kept to a minimum.
• Assets exceeding the exemption amount (CHF 5,000 per person / max. CHF 15,000 per household) must be used first.
• Persons without a residence permit and with a legally binding removal order are only entitled to emergency aid, not ordinary social assistance.
• The bonus for the third and subsequent children follows the social policy of supporting large families in Canton Nidwalden.
