Transparency creates trust. The Supplementary Benefits (EL) to AHV/IV guarantee the coverage of basic living expenses. Here we document completely and mathematically precisely how our calculator determines your entitlement in the canton of Solothurn based on federal regulations (ELG/ELV) and the cantonal guidelines of the AKSO.

1. Annual Basic Needs (2026)

Single Persons

CHF 20'670

Nationally harmonized flat-rate amount for living expenses.

Married Couples

CHF 31'005

Joint statutory basic needs for both spouses.

2. The Precise Calculation Path

1

Recognized Expenses (Needs)

The calculation adds up all legally creditable expense items of the household:

Recognized Expenses = Basic Needs + Rent (up to maximum) + Health Insurance Reference Premium + Child Allowance

Rent Maxima in Canton Solothurn (2026)

The municipalities in the canton of Solothurn fall into regions 2 and 3. Our system automatically assigns the limits by postal code (PLZ):

*Special surcharges: For shared apartments (WG), a reduced federal entitlement applies (Region 2: CHF 905/Month, Region 3: CHF 840/Month). Owner-occupied property is considered with a flat rate of CHF 3'480 p.a. Wheelchair-accessible apartments receive a surcharge of up to CHF 6'900 p.a.

Health Insurance Reference Premiums Solothurn 2026

The actual premium is credited, but at most the cantonal average premium (reference premium):

  • Adults (26 years and older): max. CHF 7'224 p.a.
  • Young Adults (19-25 years): max. CHF 5'220 p.a.
  • Children (up to 18 years): max. CHF 1'668 p.a.
2

Creditable Income

The modified income according to ELG criteria is compared to the needs:

  • 13th AHV Pension Neutralization: Since the introduction of the 13th AHV pension in 2026, it is isolated in a constitutionally compliant manner and not counted as income. Only the regular 12 monthly pensions are included in the calculation. Other forms of pension (BVG/pension fund) are fully recorded.
  • Statutory Employment Income Regulation: Gross employment income is not fully touched. A statutory deduction applies (CHF 1'000 for single persons, CHF 1'500 for married couples). From the remaining amount, **only 2/3 (66.67%)** is counted as income.
  • Asset Consumption (Allowance Deduction): Fair allowances are deducted from the net assets. The remainder is divided by the statutory divisor (1/10 for AHV pensioners, 1/15 for IV pensioners):
Category Allowance (Income Reduction)
Single Persons CHF 30'000
Married Couples CHF 50'000
Children (per child) CHF 15'000
Owner-Occupied Property (Standard) CHF 112'500 (Increase to CHF 300'000 possible for married couples/IV pensioners)
3

Result: Annual EL Pension

The statutory supplementary benefit exactly closes the mathematical coverage gap:

Annual EL Pension = [Recognized Expenses] - [Creditable Income]

If the result is above zero and all entry thresholds are met, a legal entitlement exists. Payment is made monthly (Annual EL / 12).

Important: The Statutory Asset Threshold (Entry Threshold)
An entitlement to supplementary benefits generally exists only if the adjusted net assets (excluding the value of an owner-occupied property) are below **CHF 100'000** for single persons or **CHF 200'000** for married couples. For children, this threshold increases by CHF 50'000 each.
