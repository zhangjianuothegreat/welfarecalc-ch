In Switzerland, family allowances are regulated by law (FamZG). The Canton of Valais (VS) traditionally grants higher rates compared to other cantons, as well as a degressive/progressive tiering from the 3rd child and additional one-time allowances. Our calculator uses the official 2026 CIVAF rates to break down your entitlement with absolute transparency.

1. Monthly Rates & One-time Allowances (2026)

Child Allowance (1st & 2nd child)

CHF 327 / month

Up to the age of 16 (or 20 years if incapacitated for work).

Child Allowance (from 3rd child)

CHF 435 / month

Increased rate for large families from the third eligible child.

Education Allowance (1st & 2nd child)

CHF 477 / month

From age 16 until the end of education (max. up to the 25th birthday).

Education Allowance (from 3rd child)

CHF 585 / month

Increased education rate from the third child in education.

2. The Calculation Method

1

Determining the Category

The algorithm checks the age and education status of each reported child separately:

  • 0 - 16 years (or up to 20 years if incapacitated for work): Classification in the Child Allowance category.
  • 16 - 25 years: Classification in the Education Allowance category (provided formal education or studies are proven).
2

Calculation of the Total Monthly Amount

Since the Canton of Valais applies a tiering from the 3rd child, the sum is not multiplied purely linearly, but follows a hierarchical gradation within the categories:

A) Formula for Child Allowance (childMonthly):

If the number of younger children (numChildren) is less than 3:

childMonthly = numChildren × 327

If the number of younger children (numChildren) is greater than or equal to 3:

childMonthly = (2 × 327) + ((numChildren - 2) × 435)

B) Formula for Education Allowance (educationMonthly):

If the number of young people in education (numEducation) is less than 3:

educationMonthly = numEducation × 477

If the number of young people in education (numEducation) is greater than or equal to 3:

educationMonthly = (2 × 477) + ((numEducation - 2) × 585)
Total Monthly Entitlement = childMonthly + educationMonthly
3

One-time Allowances (Birth / Adoption)

In contrast to many German-speaking cantons, Valais grants generous one-time payments for the birth or adoption of a child. The algorithm also distinguishes between single and multiple birth events:

Event Type Calculation / Amount per Child (VS)
Single Birth (numNewborns = 1) CHF 2'142 flat rate
Multiple Birth (numNewborns > 1) CHF 3'213 × number of newborns
Single Adoption (numAdoptions = 1) CHF 2'142 flat rate
Simultaneous Adoption of Multiple Children (numAdoptions > 1) CHF 3'213 × number of adopted children

*Note: For fraud prevention and algorithmic validation, our core engine performs defensive validation steps, e.g., numNewborns <= numChildren .

4

Determining the Annual Entitlement

The mathematical combination of the monthly recurring benefits with the event-dependent one-time allowances provides the definitive annual budget:

Annual Amount = (childMonthly + educationMonthly) × 12 + birthAllowance + adoptionAllowance
Entitlement & Priority Order:
According to Art. 12 FamZG, only one allowance can be claimed for the same child. If both parents are employed, the federal legal priority order (primary claim by the main earner at the child's place of residence) regulates the allocation. Payment is usually made directly via the employer's monthly salary.
