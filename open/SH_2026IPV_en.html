Trust through transparency. Here we mathematically disclose how our calculator determines your potential premium reduction (IPV) in the Canton of Schaffhausen. This algorithm strictly follows the official guidelines of the SVA Schaffhausen.

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Deductible / Co-payment

15.0 %

Share of assessable income deemed affordable for health insurance.

Asset Depletion Rate

15.0 %

Percentage of taxable wealth added to income.

Max. Income Threshold

CHF 80'000

If LNA exceeds this amount, eligibility is completely lost.

2. The Calculation Path

1

Assessable Income (LNA)

First, we determine your economic capacity according to cantonal specifications:

LNA = Max(0, [Annual net income] + ( [Taxable assets] × 15%) + [Additional components] - [Allowance/Deduction] )
Category Allowance (Deduction)
Single person (Individual) CHF 4'500
Married couples / Cohabitants CHF 9'000
Per child / Young adult in education + CHF 4'500
2

Income Threshold Check

The canton limits access to IPV through a strict ceiling on assessable income:

If LNA > CHF 80'000 → Entitlement = CHF 0
3

Total Reference Premium 2026

The reference premiums are the benchmark values set by the Canton of Schaffhausen for 2026:

Category Reference Premium (Annual)
Adults (26 years and older) CHF 5'320
Young adults (19-25 in education) CHF 3'724
Children (0-18 years) CHF 1'220
Total Reference Premium = ∑ (Individual reference premiums of all household members)
4

Deductible & Preliminary Reduction

The statutory deductible (15%) is subtracted from the total reference premium:

Deductible amount = Max(0, LNA) × 15%
Preliminary reduction = Max(0, Total Reference Premium - Deductible amount)
5

Minimum Guarantees Review (KVG)

For children and young adults in education, statutory minimum protection applies for low and middle incomes:

Category Guaranteed statutory portion
Children (benchmark protection) 80% of child reference premium (CHF 976)
Youth in education 50% of youth reference premium (CHF 1'862)
Minimum floor = (∑ children × CHF 976) + (∑ young adults × CHF 1'862)
Adjusted reduction = Max(Preliminary reduction, Minimum floor)
6

Minimum Payout & Cap

Finally, rounding occurs along with the final cap determined by the actual premium paid:

Final annual reduction = Min(Adjusted reduction [rounded to nearest franc], Actual annual premium)

De minimis threshold: If the calculated annual entitlement is below CHF 100 , the payment is completely waived (Reduction = 0).
