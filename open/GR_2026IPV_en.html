We believe in algorithmic transparency. Here you will learn how our system calculates your entitlement to premium reduction in the Canton of Grisons. This logic is based on the KPVG GR and the implementing provisions of the SVA GR.

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Deductible Rate

8.0% — 12.0%

Staggered based on the amount of the excess.

Wealth portion

10.0%

Portion of taxable wealth counted as income.

2. Calculation Path

1

Relevant Income (LNA)

The LNA is calculated specifically in the canton of GR:

LNA = [Net income] + [10% Wealth] - [Pillar 2 / 3a contributions*]

*Important: Unlike other cantons, contributions to retirement provisions are deductible in GR.

Category Basic Deduction
Single persons CHF 12'000
Married couples / Partners CHF 18'000
Per child (school age / education) + CHF 6'000
2

Reference Premiums 2026 (Region 2)

Grisons is divided into 3 regions. Here are the amounts for standard Region 2:

Adults CHF 5'820 / year
Young adults CHF 4'280 / year
Children (0-18) CHF 1'380 / year
3

Excess & Deductible

The deductible increases progressively:

Excess = LNA - Total deductions
Deductible amount = Excess × [8% up to 12%]

The rate is 8% for small excess amounts and increases up to 12% for excess above CHF 40'000.

4

Special Child Relief

If the LNA is below CHF 85'000, the child deductible is further reduced:

LNA Range Relief (deductible reduction factor)
Up to CHF 55'000 100% relief (Factor 0.0)
CHF 55'001 – 65'000 Partial relief (sliding scale)
CHF 65'001 – 80'000 40% relief (Factor 0.6)
CHF 80'001 – 85'000 Reduced relief (linear tapering)

* Full details available in SVA guidelines. The relief factor multiplies the standard child deductible.

Important note:
Subsidies below CHF 100 per year will not be paid out. The maximum subsidy is always limited by the actual premium paid.
