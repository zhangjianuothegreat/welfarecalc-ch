In the Canton of Glarus, the individual premium reduction (IPV) is based on economic performance. Below we disclose the mathematical steps our calculator uses to determine your eligibility.

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Income Burden

9.0 % — 14.0 %

Progressive rate depending on relevant income.

Asset Assessment

10.0 %

Share of taxable net assets (above allowance).

2. The Calculation Path

1

Relevant Income (LNA)

The LNA is determined very precisely in the Canton of GL:

Net income + 10% (Assets - Allowance*) + Adjustments** - Child deductions

*Allowance: CHF 50,000 (single persons) / CHF 100,000 (married couples).
**Adjustments: Including imputed rental value, mortgage interest, and maintenance payments.

Category Income Deduction
Per child / vocational training CHF 5,000
2

Reference Premiums 2026

These cantonal benchmarks form the basis for relief:

Adults CHF 5,447 / year
Young adults (19-25) CHF 3,896 / year
Children (0-18) CHF 1,500 / year
3

Determining the Retention Rate

The affordable personal contribution increases with the LNA:

LNA Threshold (CHF) Rate (%)
up to 40,000 9.0 %
40,001 - 50,000 10.0 %
50,001 - 60,000 11.0 %
60,001 - 70,000 12.0 %
70,001 - 80,000 13.0 %
over 80,000 14.0 %
4

Final Entitlement & Minimum Guarantee

First, the difference is calculated:

Base Entitlement = [Total Reference Premiums] - ([LNA] × Rate)

For families with income below CHF 85,000, the minimum guarantee applies:

Category Guarantee
Children 80% of the reference premium
Adolescents in education 50% of the reference premium
Important thresholds:
Amounts below CHF 100 per year are not paid out in the Canton of GL. Furthermore, the entitlement is always limited by the premium actually billed.
