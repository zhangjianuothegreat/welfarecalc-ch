Emergency assistance (according to Art. 12 of the Federal Constitution) for persons required to leave the country or persons with a legally binding removal decision is strictly handled as an absolute flat rate in the Canton of Zug. Here we disclose the verified mathematical logic based on the directives of the Cantonal Social Welfare Office of Zug.

1. Legal Emergency Assistance Parameters (Zug 2026)

Basic Daily Rate (Single Person)

CHF 12.00 / Day

Applies to a single adult person in a collective accommodation.

Couple Graduation

CHF 18.00 / Day

Fixed amount for two married or partnered adults living together.

Child Fixed Amount

CHF 7.00 / Day per Child

Linear amount per minor child (no degressive reduction).

2. The Official Calculation Method (Step by Step)

1

Determination of the Adult Daily Rate

The Canton of Zug does not calculate adult rates using percentage factors, but uses an absolute cantonal amount matrix:

Number of Adults = 1: CHF 12.00 / Day
Number of Adults = 2: CHF 18.00 / Day
Number of Adults ≥ 3: CHF 18.00 + (Number of Adults - 2) × CHF 8.50 / Day

Note: For large family households in collective accommodations, a reduced individual rate of CHF 8.50 is applied from the third adult onwards.

2

Add Child Fixed Amount

For each child registered in the household (under 18 years) or a support element still in education, a fixed daily rate of CHF 7.00 is added.

Total Daily Amount = Adult Daily Rate + (Number of Children × CHF 7.00)
3

Monthly Projection & Administrative Rounding

The transformation to the monthly entitlement is carried out using the Swiss standard factor of 30.42 days. The resulting value is strictly rounded commercially to the next full franc (1.00 CHF) .

Monthly Entitlement = ROUND( (Total Daily Amount × 30.42) ; 0 )
Rounding Practice Canton Zug Example
Commercially to exactly 1.00 CHF 421.49 → 421.00 CHF
Commercially to exactly 1.00 CHF 584.50 → 585.00 CHF
Cantonal Special Features for Emergency Assistance in Zug (ZG):
• Priority of Benefits in Kind: The calculated amounts represent the pocket money for food and hygiene. Accommodation and emergency medical care are fully provided as benefits in kind by the canton.
• Asset Limits: A theoretical entitlement expires if liquid funds or assets exceed the cantonal allowance.
• No Large Family Degression: The child allowance remains stable at CHF 7.00/day even from the 3rd child.
