How is social welfare calculated in the Canton of Zug? To strengthen the trust of our users, we disclose here the complete algorithm behind welfarecalc.ch. It is based on the Social Welfare Act of the Canton of Zug (SHG ZG) as well as the SKOS guidelines 2026.

1. Asset Check (The Entry Threshold)

Allowance Single Person

CHF 10'000

Plus CHF 4'000 per child or person in education.

Allowance Married Couples

CHF 20'000

Maximum total allowance capped at CHF 25'000 per support unit.

2. The Calculation Path (Monthly)

1

Basic Needs for Living (GBL)

The GBL covers the costs for food, clothing, and general household management:

Household Size Amount per Month (2026)
1 Person CHF 1'061
2 Persons CHF 1'624
3 Persons CHF 1'974
4 Persons CHF 2'271
5 Persons CHF 2'568
Each additional person + CHF 216
2

Housing Costs & Health

In contrast to other cantons, the Canton of Zug applies uniform maximum rent limits without regional gradations:

Household Size Maximum allowable gross rent (Month)
1 Person CHF 1'500
2 Persons CHF 1'800
3 Persons CHF 2'100
4 Persons CHF 2'400
5+ Persons CHF 2'700
Recognized housing costs = Min(Actual rent, Cantonal maximum)

Health insurance: Deduction of the effective premium (KVG basic insurance) minus the individual premium subsidy (IPV). Negative values are limited to 0.

3

Income Deduction & Allowances

An income allowance (EFB) is deducted from the gross earned income to maintain the motivation to work:

Countable Income = Net income - EFB (20% of gross salary, max. CHF 400 per pers.)

Other income (pensions, alimony, child allowances) is counted as 100% revenue without deductions.

4

Result: Monthly Entitlement

The difference calculation determines the monthly support:

Entitlement = (GBL + Recognized Rent + HI Premium*) - (Countable Income)

*HI Premium = Net premium after deduction of the cantonal premium subsidy (IPV).

Subsidiarity Principle & Integration in the Canton of Zug:
If liquid assets exceed the permissible allowance, there is no entitlement to social welfare from the outset. Persons who fulfill an employment obligation or provide special integration services can additionally receive an integration allowance (IZU) of CHF 100 per month.
