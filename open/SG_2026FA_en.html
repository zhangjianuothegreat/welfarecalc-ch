In Switzerland, family allowances are regulated by law (FamZG). Our calculator uses the specific cantonal rates of the Canton of St. Gallen for 2026 to accurately determine your entitlement. Transparency is important to us.

1. Monthly Rates SG (2026)

Child allowance

CHF 245 / month

For children from 0 up to the age of 16.

Education allowance

CHF 298 / month

From age 16 until the end of education (max. 25 years).

2. The Calculation Process

1

Determining the category and checking eligibility

The algorithm checks the age, status and legal minimum requirements for each child:

  • Minimum income of parents: To be entitled to full allowances, the parent receiving the allowance must have an AHV-liable income of at least CHF 630 / month (CHF 7'560 / year).
  • 0 - 16 years: Entitlement to child allowance (CHF 245).
  • 16 - 25 years: Entitlement to education allowance (CHF 298), provided that the child is in recognized education or training and the child's personal annual income does not exceed CHF 30,240.
  • Children with disabilities: Entitlement to child allowance up to the age of 20 if unable to work.
2

Calculation of the monthly amount

The Canton of St. Gallen uses a linear mathematical model without progressive scaling by number of children or additional age steps:

Monthly sum = (Number of children × CHF 245) + (Number in education/training × CHF 298)

*Important: In the Canton of St. Gallen, there is no increase for large families (from the 3rd child onward) and no separate age-related tiers (as in ZH or LU).

3

One-time allowances (birth/adoption)

Unlike some other cantons, the Canton of St. Gallen grants no one-time birth or adoption allowances.

Type of allowanceAmount (SG)
Birth allowanceCHF 0

4

Determining the annual entitlement

Payment is made monthly via the employer or directly through the Family Compensation Fund (SVA SG):

Annual amount = (Monthly total entitlement) × 12
Eligibility & Order of priority:
Allowances for the same child are paid only once. If both parents are gainfully employed, the statutory order of priority according to Art. 12 FamZG strictly determines the primary entitlement (based on place of employment, custody, and higher AHV income).
