How is social welfare calculated in the Canton of Zurich? To strengthen the trust of our users, we disclose here the complete algorithm behind welfarecalc.ch. It is based on the Social Welfare Act of the Canton of Zurich (SHG ZH) as well as the SKOS guidelines 2026.

1. Asset Check (The Entry Threshold)

Allowance Individual

CHF 6'000

Additional CHF 3'000 per child or person in education.

Allowance Married Couples

CHF 12'000

Maximum total allowance capped at CHF 15'000 per household.

2. The Calculation Path (Monthly)

1

Basic Needs for Living (GBL)

The GBL covers the costs for food, clothing, and household management:

Household Size Amount per Month (2026)
1 Person CHF 1'061
2 Persons CHF 1'624
3 Persons (e.g., couple + 1 child < 11) CHF 2'015
Additional Adult in Household + CHF 400
Child Gradation (depending on age) CHF 391 to CHF 1'061
2

Housing Costs by Rent Regions

Recognition of effective rental costs including ancillary costs up to the cantonal maximum based on three postal code regions:

Recognized Rent = Min(Effective Rent, Regional Maximum)
Household Size Region A (City of Zurich/Winterthur) Region B (Agglomeration) Region C (Rural)
1 Person CHF 1'400 CHF 1'300 CHF 1'200
2 Persons CHF 1'700 CHF 1'600 CHF 1'500
3 Persons CHF 2'000 CHF 1'900 CHF 1'800
4 Persons CHF 2'300 CHF 2'200 CHF 2'100
5+ Persons CHF 2'600 CHF 2'500 CHF 2'400
3

Health Insurance & IPV Offset

The compulsory health insurance (KVG) is recognized, whereby the cantonal individual premium subsidy (IPV) is directly deducted:

Recognized Health Insurance Premium = Max(0, Effective Premium - Monthly IPV)

*Note: A remaining IPV surplus is fully counted as income on the revenue side.

4

Income Assessment & Earned Income Deduction

An allowance is deducted from the net earned income to maintain the work incentive according to SKOS:

Countable Income = Net Income - Earned Income Deduction (20%)

Other income such as pensions, alimony, or child allowances are counted as 100% revenue.

5

Result: Monthly Entitlement

The final social welfare payment results from the mathematical deficit in needs:

Entitlement = Max(0, [GBL + Rent + Health Insurance + Integration Allowance] - [Income + Asset Depletion])
Note on Integration & Asset Depletion:
- Employed persons or persons providing integration services may receive an integration allowance (IZU) of CHF 100.
- Assets exceeding the allowance are linearly counted as monthly asset depletion over a time horizon of 10 years (divisor 120).
