Important legal notice regarding the Federal Constitution (Art. 12 BV):
Emergency aid (Aide d'urgence) is constitutionally not regular social welfare (Aide sociale/RI). It serves exclusively to bridge acute emergencies to preserve human dignity. In the Canton of Vaud, this benefit is administered by EVAM (Etablissement vaudois d'accueil des migrants) and strictly prioritized as a benefit in kind (accommodation, collective meals, hygiene items). Cash benefits only arise subsidiarily.

1. Legal Parameters (Cash Benefit Approach 2026)

2. The Mathematical Calculation Process

The determination of the paid emergency aid in the Canton of Vaud is carried out step by step without canton-specific scaling factors:

1

Determination of the total daily rate (per capita share)

Since no social family structures are mapped in the cantonal emergency aid Vaud (case law on the constitutional minimum assistance), all household members are aggregated linearly regardless of age.

Daily budget = (Number of adults + Number of children) × CHF 9.50
2

Projection based on actual calendar days

In contrast to ordinary social welfare or supplementary benefits (EL), Vaud emergency aid does not have a flat monthly factor of 30.42 days. The payout is based exactly on the days of the relevant calendar month (28, 29, 30 or 31 days).

Monthly amount (gross) = Daily budget × Calendar days (e.g., 30 or 31)
3

Commercial rounding

The calculated monthly amount is rounded to the standard commercial decimal place (0.01 CHF) according to the EVAM payout standard.

Note on health insurance coverage: The premium for compulsory health insurance (KVG) as well as necessary acute medical treatments are covered directly by EVAM within the framework of flat-rate contracts and do not appear in the individual cash budget.
