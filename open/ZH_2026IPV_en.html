Trust through transparency. Here we disclose how our calculator determines your potential premium reduction (IPV) in the Canton of Zurich. This algorithm is strictly based on the legal requirements of the SVA Zurich and the cantonal ordinance for the year 2026.

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Deduction Rate (Deductible)

Individuals: 8.4 %
Families: 10.5 %

Percentage of the relevant income considered reasonable as a personal contribution.

Asset Consumption Rate

10.0 %

Portion of the taxable net assets (after deduction of the allowance) that is added to the income.

2. The Calculation Path

1

Relevant Income (LNA)

The relevant income determines your economic capacity and is based on the tax data:

LNA = [Net Income*] + [10% × (Taxable Assets - Asset Allowance)]

*Note: The Net Income is adjusted for certain deductions (e.g., pillar 2 purchase contributions) according to cantonal guidelines.

Marital Status / Category Asset Allowance
Single persons / Single-parent families CHF 75'000
Married couples / Couples CHF 150'000
2

Official Reference Premiums 2026

The Canton of Zurich divides the municipalities into 3 premium regions. The IPV calculation is based on 70% of the average premium (reference premium):

Region Adults (from 26) Young Adults (19-25 in education) Children (0-18)
Region 1 (e.g., City of Zurich) CHF 7'704 / year CHF 5'532 / year CHF 1'854 / year
Region 2 (e.g., Winterthur) CHF 7'020 / year CHF 5'040 / year CHF 1'686 / year
Region 3 (Rural municipalities) CHF 6'540 / year CHF 4'692 / year CHF 1'566 / year
3

Calculation of the Personal Contribution (Splitting Special Rule)

The personal contribution (deductible) is determined differently depending on the income level:

A. Standard Calculation (Normal Earners)

Personal Contribution = Max([Total Reference Premium] × 30%, LNA × Deduction Rate)

B. Special Regulation for Low-Income Families

If a family (with children or young adults in education) falls below the cantonal income limits ( CHF 70'500 without education / CHF 94'000 with education), the statutory child protection applies via the splitting procedure:

  • Children: Bear a fixed personal contribution of only 20% of their reference premium.
  • Young adults in education: Bear a reduced personal contribution of 50% of their reference premium.
  • Adults: Bear the remaining income deduction, but at least 30% of the adult reference premium.
4

Final Entitlement (IPV Benefit)

Theoretical Entitlement = Max(0, [Total Reference Premiums] - [Total Personal Contribution])

The effective IPV benefit is capped by the actual annual premium paid to the health insurance company. The payment is made monthly (rounded to the nearest franc).

Observe the absolute asset hardship limit:
In the Canton of Zurich, any entitlement to IPV automatically expires if the taxable net assets exceed CHF 150'000 (for single persons) or CHF 300'000 (for married couples) – regardless of how low the current income is.
