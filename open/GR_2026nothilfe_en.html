Transparency builds trust. In the Canton of Graubünden (GR), emergency aid for rejected asylum seekers is calculated using a specific degressive model. Here we disclose the full algorithm that forms the basis of our calculations on welfarecalc.ch.

1. Key Parameters (GR 2026)

Base Daily Rate (Single)

CHF 9.00 / day

Collective accommodation incl. basic needs (pocket money/meals).

Couple Coefficient

Factor 1.65

Reduced rates for two cohabiting adults.

Child Factor

Factor 0.52

Additional need per child compared to the base rate.

2. Calculation Steps (Step by Step)

1

Calculation of Adult Need

The Canton of GR uses a degression factor of 1.65 for couples. This means the second person receives 65% of the single rate.

If adults = 1: CHF 9.00 / day
If adults > 1: (9.00 + (number of adults - 1) × 9.00 × 0.65)
2

Calculation of Child Supplements

For each child, a basic amount of 52% of the adult rate is added. Additionally, GR grants a bonus of 10% of the base rate from the 3rd child onward.

Child base: CHF 4.68 (9.00 × 0.52)
Bonus from 3rd child: + CHF 0.90 (9.00 × 0.10)
Total child = (children × 4.68) + (MAX(0; children-2) × 0.90)
3

Monthly Extrapolation & Rounding

The total daily amount is multiplied by the monthly factor 30.42 and rounded to the next full franc.

Monthly entitlement = ROUND( (Total daily amount × 30.42) ; 0 )
Graubünden Specifics:
• Accommodation type: These rates apply to accommodation in cantonal collective centers.
• Benefits in kind priority: Emergency aid is primarily provided in kind (accommodation/food).
• Legal basis: According to the cantonal asylum ordinance and directives of the GR Migration Office.
