Trust is created through transparency. Here we disclose the complete algorithm that drives the calculations for the canton of Uri on welfarecalc.ch. The logic is based on the Uri Social Welfare Act (SHG UR), the associated ordinance, and the SKOS guidelines 2026.

1. Asset Check (The Entry Threshold)

Allowance Individual

CHF 4'000

Plus CHF 2'000 per child or person in education.

Allowance Married Couples

CHF 8'000

The allowance increases purely according to household structure (no flat maximum cap).

2. The Calculation Path (Monthly)

1

Basic Living Allowance (GBL)

The GBL 2026 in the canton of Uri strictly follows the current SKOS specifications for food, clothing, and household management:

Household Size Amount per Month (2026)
1 person CHF 1'061
2 persons CHF 1'624
3 persons CHF 1'974
4 persons CHF 2'271
5 persons CHF 2'568
Each additional person + CHF 216
2

Housing Costs & Health

Recognition of actual housing and health insurance costs, taking into account cantonal maximum limits:

Recognized housing costs = Min(Actual rent, Regional maximum per municipality)
Health insurance requirement = Actual premium (KVG) - Individual premium subsidy (IPV)

*Note: The rent guidelines in the canton of Uri depend on the respective residential municipality (e.g., Altdorf vs. mountain municipalities). Our calculator automatically incorporates these regional differences.

3

Income & Earned Income Allowance (EFB)

To maintain work incentives, employed persons are granted an allowance on their net income:

Countable Income = Net income - EFB (according to SKOS scale, max. CHF 400 per person)

All secondary income, alimony, pensions, and IPV subsidies are offset against income at 100%.

4

Result: Monthly Entitlement

The definitive monthly entitlement to economic assistance results from the shortfall in basic needs:

Entitlement = (GBL + Rent + Health Insurance + SIL*) - Countable Income

*SIL = Situation-related benefits (e.g., documented health or employment-related additional costs).

Asset Depletion in the Canton of Uri:
There is no automatic "monthly partial depletion over 10 years". If the household's free assets exceed the statutory asset allowance, according to the principle of subsidiarity, there is no entitlement to social welfare. The excess assets must be fully used up before receiving benefits.
