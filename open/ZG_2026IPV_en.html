Trust through transparency. Here we disclose how our calculator determines your potential Individual Premium Reduction (IPV) in the Canton of Zug. This algorithm is strictly based on the legal foundations of the Canton of Zug (BGS 842.6) as well as the guidelines of the Zug Compensation Office.

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Deductible Factor

8.0 %

Proportion of the relevant income considered reasonable for health insurance premiums.

Asset Inclusion

10.0 %

Percentage of taxable net assets added as countable income.

2. The Calculation Path

1

Relevant Income (LNA)

First, the household's economic capacity is calculated. In the Canton of Zug, 10% of the taxable net assets is added as an income component:

LNA = Max(0, [Taxable Net Income] + [Voluntary 2nd Pillar] + [Pillar 3a] + ([Taxable Assets] × 10%) - [Child Deduction])
Deduction Category Amount (per person)
Lump-sum deduction per child / young person in education category CHF 8'500
Income Limit: If the calculated LNA exceeds the amount of CHF 89'900, the legal entitlement to IPV expires completely.
2

Check of the Asset Exclusion Limit

In addition to the income test, a strict asset upper limit (Hard Limit) applies as an exclusion criterion:

Household Type Absolute Asset Limit
Single person CHF 150'000
Married couples / Registered partnerships CHF 300'000

If the taxable assets exceed these cantonal thresholds, there is no entitlement to subsidies.

3

Cantonal Reference Premiums 2026

The reference premiums represent the cantonal standard average premiums for the calculation:

Category Reference Premium (annual)
Adults (26 years and older) CHF 5'916 / year
Young adults (19-25 years, in education) CHF 4'140 / year
Children (0-18 years) CHF 1'392 / year
4

Calculation of the Individual Deductible

The reasonable personal contribution is based on the relevant income (LNA):

Deductible = LNA × 8.0%
5

Determination of the Theoretical Subsidy

The theoretical subsidy amount results from the difference between the total reference premiums and the individual deductible:

Theoretical Subsidy = Max(0, [Total cantonal reference premiums] - [Deductible])
6

Application of Minimum Guarantees (Child & Youth Protection)

To relieve families financially, the statutory minimum guarantee for children and young people in education applies for low and middle incomes:

Minimum Guarantee = (Number of children × CHF 1'392 × 80%) + (Number of young people in educ. × CHF 4'140 × 50%) Theoretical Subsidy Correction = Max(Theoretical Subsidy, Minimum Guarantee)

If the regularly calculated household subsidy is lower than this statutory lower limit of the child and youth lump sums, the amount of the minimum guarantee is granted instead.

7

Payout Threshold and Final Limitation

The final annual IPV payout is subject to a minimum limit and is capped by the actually paid premium:

1. Payout Threshold: If (Theoretical Subsidy Correction < CHF 100) → Entitlement = 0 CHF 2. Final Annual Subsidy = Min(Round(Theoretical Subsidy Correction), Actual annual premium) 3. Monthly Credit = Round(Final Annual Subsidy / 12)
