Trust through transparency. We disclose how our calculator determines your potential premium reduction (IPV) in the Canton of Basel-Stadt. This algorithm strictly follows the legal provisions of the Basel-Stadt KVO.

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Income Groups

Degressive scaling of IPV contributions.

2. The Calculation Pathway

1

Determination of Asset Allowance

First, the household's taxable net assets are analyzed. If assets exceed the statutory allowances for 2026, the excess amount is partially added to the income.

Household TypeAllowance (2026)
Single personCHF 50'000
Married couples / Registered partnershipsCHF 100'000
Supplement per child / person in initial education+ CHF 15'000
2

Calculation of Relevant Income (LNA)

The income relevant for premium reduction (LNA) is derived from net income plus a 10% depletion of excess assets, minus verifiable alimony paid.

AssetSurplus = Max(0, TaxableAssets - Allowance)
LNA = NetIncome + (AssetSurplus × 10%) - AlimonyPaid
3

Classification into 22 Income Groups

Depending on the calculated LNA and the exact household size (number of adults + children), the household is placed into a degressive table (Groups 1 to max 22). The interval thresholds increase uniformly by CHF 1'250 per group.

Household Size (Persons)Income Limit Group 1 (Min)Income Limit Group 22 (Max)
1 person (e.g., single)CHF 23'125CHF 49'375
2 persons (e.g., couple)CHF 37'000CHF 63'250
3 personsCHF 47'000CHF 73'250
4 personsCHF 55'000CHF 81'250

4

Determination of Cantonal Flat-Rate Contribution

The entitlement is not reduced based on the effective insurance plan chosen, but credited as a fixed cantonal flat-rate lump sum. This incentivizes cost-efficient insurance models (HMO/family doctor).

MonthlySubsidy_Total = (Adults × Rate_A) + (In_Education × Rate_B) + (Children × Rate_C)

*Note: If the calculated LNA exceeds the maximum threshold of Group 22, the IPV entitlement for 2026 is completely void (entitlement = CHF 0).

Key Feature in Canton Basel-Stadt:
The system in Basel-Stadt is strongly degressive. Households in Group 1 receive the maximum reference premium subsidy (up to 100% of the standardized basic premium), while middle-income classes receive progressively less support until entitlement fully ceases from Group 22.
