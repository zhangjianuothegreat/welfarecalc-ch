In the Canton of Lucerne (LU), family allowances are governed by cantonal law as well as federal law (FamZG). Our calculator implements the specific age-based differentiation and one-time allowances of the Canton of Lucerne for 2026.

1. Monthly Rates & One-Time Payments (2026)

Child allowance (up to 12)

CHF 215 / month

For children up to the completed 12th year of age.

Child allowance (from 12)

CHF 260 / month

Increased rate from age 12 up to age 16.

Education allowance

CHF 268 / month

From age 16 until the end of education (max. age 25).

Birth / Adoption

CHF 1,075

One-time allowance per child in the Canton of Lucerne.

2. Calculation Method

1

Age-based differentiation

Unlike many other cantons, Lucerne differentiates the child allowance after the 12th birthday:

  • 0 - 12 years: CHF 215 per child per month.
  • 12 - 16 years: CHF 260 per child per month.
  • 16 - 25 years: CHF 268 per child per month (in education).
2

Monthly amount calculation

The algorithm sums the amounts based on the age reference date:

(n1 × 215) + (n2 × 260) + (n education × 268)

where n1 = children < 12 years and n2 = children between 12-16 years.

3

One-time allowances

Lucerne grants a birth or adoption allowance. This is added to the total amount in the year of the event.

Type of allowance Amount (LU)
Birth allowance CHF 1,075
Adoption allowance CHF 1,075
4

Determining the annual entitlement

The formula for the total year including one-time benefits:

Annual amount = (Monthly sum × 12) + One-time allowances
Note on payment:
Allowances are generally paid out together with the salary. Self-employed persons in the Canton of Lucerne settle accounts directly with the compensation fund WAS Lucerne.
