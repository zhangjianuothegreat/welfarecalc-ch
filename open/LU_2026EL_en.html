Transparency is the foundation of our calculator. Here we disclose the mathematical logic behind the EL calculation for the canton of Lucerne. Our algorithm strictly follows the Federal Law (ELG) and the cantonal provisions of WAS Lucerne.

2

Countable Income

Next, the calculator determines the countable income:

  • Pensions: AHV/IV benefits (The 13th AHV pension is neutralised).
  • Earned income: 2/3 of income is counted after deducting allowances.
  • Asset depletion: (Net assets - exemption amount) / 10 (old-age pension) or 1/15 (disability insurance).

3

Result: EL Entitlement

The difference between recognised needs and countable income yields the EL amount:

Annual EL = [Recognised Needs] - [Countable Income]

If the result is negative, there is no entitlement to EL.

Important: Asset threshold
In the canton of Lucerne, an entitlement to EL exists only if net assets are below CHF 100,000 (single persons) or CHF 200,000 (married couples). Owner-occupied residential property is not counted towards this threshold.
