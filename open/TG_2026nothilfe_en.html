The canton of Thurgau (TG) regulates the payment of constitutional emergency aid (Art. 12 BV) primarily through in-kind accommodation benefits and an extremely restrictive daily pocket money for cash needs. This disclosure report documents the exact algorithmic mapping of these guidelines at the core of our platform.

1. Legal System Parameters (Canton TG)

Base Daily Rate (1st Adult)

CHF 8.00

Couple Factor (Degression)

1.68

Additional Factor per Child

0.52

Time Factor (Monthly Average)

30.42 Days

2. Step-by-Step Calculation Process

1

Calculation of the Adult Component

A single adult person receives the fixed daily rate. For married couples or life partnerships in the same collective accommodation, the cantonal degression factor (couple factor) applies to save on household synergies.

If Adults = 1: Daily_Rate_Adults = CHF 8.00
If Adults = 2: Daily_Rate_Adults = 8.00 + (1 × 8.00 × 1.68) = CHF 21.44
2

Addition of the Minor Flat Rate

For each child living in the household, a reduced fixed factor is applied. In contrast to other cantons, there is no degressive scale from the 3rd child onwards in the canton of Thurgau.

Daily_Rate_Children = Number_Children × (8.00 × 0.52) = Number_Children × CHF 4.16
3

Annual Average Monthly Conversion & Rounding

From the addition of the daily partial rates, the monthly equivalent is calculated based on the average month length of a year (365 days / 12 months = 30.42). The cantonal protocol stipulates commercial rounding to the nearest **whole franc**.

4. Important Restrictions and Legal Deviations

  • Pure Cash Benefit: The calculated contributions represent purely pocket money/food money. Accommodation, basic clothing, and emergency medical care are provided directly as in-kind benefits in the collective accommodations.
  • Asylum Payment Card (New 2026): According to the government council decision, the payment of these amounts in the canton of Thurgau will gradually be made cashless via a state-controlled debit card with limited withdrawal function.
  • Children Subject to Compulsory Education: No additional special educational benefits are paid out in cash; educational entitlements are provided directly in kind within the framework of accommodation in cooperation with the local school communities.
