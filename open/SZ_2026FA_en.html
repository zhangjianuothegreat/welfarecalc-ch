The Canton of Schwyz (SZ) regulates family allowances according to the federal law (FamZG) and cantonal provisions. Our transparency calculator uses the official statutory rates of the Canton of Schwyz for 2026 to guarantee a mathematically precise and comprehensible calculation.

1. Monthly Rates SZ (2026)

Child Allowance

CHF 230 / Month

Valid for children from 0 up to the completed 16th year of age.

Education Allowance

CHF 280 / Month

From 16 years up to the end of education (max. 25 years).

2. The Calculation Method

1

Determination of the Category

The algorithm classifies each child based on age and education status:

  • 0 - 16 years: Full entitlement to the ordinary child allowance (CHF 230).
  • 16 - 25 years: Entitlement to the education allowance (CHF 280), provided there is recognized proof of education and the child's annual earned income does not exceed CHF 29,400.
  • Disabled children: Entitlement to the child allowance (CHF 230) extended until the 20th year of age.
2

Calculation of the Monthly Amount

The Canton of Schwyz applies a linear tariff model. There is no increase or gradation from the 3rd child onwards (no large family allowance):

Monthly total = (Number of children × CHF 230) + (Number in education × CHF 280)

*Note: The rates apply uniformly from the first child regardless of family size.

3

One-time Allowances (Birth/Adoption)

The Canton of Schwyz grants a one-time birth allowance, but provides no adoption allowance in cantonal law.

Allowance Type Amount (SZ)
Birth Allowance CHF 1,000 (per newborn; multiplied accordingly for multiple births)
Adoption Allowance CHF 0
4

Determination of the Annual Entitlement

The monthly entitlements are extrapolated to the year and one-time allowances are mathematically added at the end:

Annual amount = (Monthly basic total × 12) + (Number of newborns × CHF 1,000)
Subsidiarity & Priority Order:
There is only one entitlement per child. If both parents are employed or work in different cantons, the legal priority order according to Art. 12 FamZG determines the primary claim. Any differential payments (difference allowances) are calculated if the entitlement of the second eligible person would be higher in another canton.
