Trust through transparency. Here we disclose how our calculator determines your potential premium reduction in the Canton of Thurgau (TG). Unlike income-percentage models, the Thurgau calculation logic is fundamentally based on the tax model of the simple tax at 100%.

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Determining Factor

Simple Tax (100%)

Classification is done directly via the basic tax amount, not via net income.

Asset Limit

CHF 0 (Absolute)

The taxable assets of the provisional or definitive assessment must be CHF 0.

2. The Calculation Path

1

Exclusion Criterion & Asset Check

First, the absolute asset barrier is checked. The system requires:

If [Taxable Assets] > 0 → No Entitlement (Entitlement = 0)

Note: In the Canton of Thurgau, any taxable assets above CHF 0 lead to a complete loss of IPV entitlement.

2

Category Classification for Adults

If assets are CHF 0, the simple cantonal tax at 100% (according to the provisional tax data of the previous year) determines the IPV category and the corresponding fixed lump-sum reduction per adult:

Category Simple Tax at 100% (CHF) Fixed IPV Contribution (CHF / Year)
Category A 0.00 to 400.00 CHF 3'408
Category B 400.01 to 600.00 CHF 2'556
Category C 600.01 to 800.00 CHF 1'704
No Entitlement > 800.00 CHF 0
[Subsidy Total Adults] = Number of Adults × Fixed IPV Contribution of the Category
3

Entitlement for Children (0 - 18 years)

In the Canton of Thurgau, children receive a separate, independent lump sum decoupled from the adult contribution. The upper limit of the simple tax is significantly higher for families:

If [Simple Tax at 100%] ≤ 1'600.00 CHF:
[Subsidy Total Children] = Number of Children × CHF 1'236 / Year

If the simple tax at 100% exceeds CHF 1'600, the child entitlement is completely forfeited.

4

Entitlement for Young Adults in Education (19 - 25 years)

For young adults who are in a legally recognized education (apprenticeship or studies) in 2026, a percentage cap applies based on the cantonal average premium:

Applies only if [Simple Tax at 100%] ≤ 800.00 CHF:
Contribution per person = 50% of the effective KVG premium, limited to max. 50% of the cantonal average premium (CHF 4'752).
Maximum Subsidy = CHF 2'376 / Year (CHF 198 / Month)
5

Payout Limitation and De Minimis

The final annual IPV entitlement results from summing up the qualified household members, but is subject to two strict mathematical checks:

1. Effective Capping: Total Entitlement = Min(Calculated Entitlement, Actually Paid KVG Premiums)
2. Minimum Payout: If Total Entitlement < CHF 30 → Entitlement = CHF 0
Important procedural note for Thurgau:
The Canton of Thurgau operates a proactive application process. Based on the provisional tax data in spring (March/April), the municipalities automatically identify potentially eligible persons and send them the pre-filled application form by mail. This must be checked, signed, and submitted by December 31, 2026 at the latest. Those who do not receive forms (e.g., due to moving or a massive drop in income) must actively request them from the municipality or online.
