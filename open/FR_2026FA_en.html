The canton of Fribourg (FR) supports families through a progressive system where allowances increase from the third child onward. Additionally, the canton grants one-time birth and adoption allowances. Our algorithm is based on the cantonal implementing provisions of the Federal Act on Family Allowances (FamZG).

1. Monthly Rates Fribourg (2026)

Child Allowance

CHF 265 - 285

1st & 2nd child: 265 CHF | From 3rd child: 285 CHF

Education Allowance

CHF 325 - 345

1st & 2nd child: 325 CHF | From 3rd child: 345 CHF

2. Calculation Steps

1

Determination of allowance type

Each child is classified according to age and educational status:

  • 0 - 16 years: Child allowance (basic).
  • 16 - 25 years: Education allowance (upon proof of enrollment in education/training).
  • Disabled children: Child allowance until the age of 20.
2

Progressive tier calculation

The algorithm takes into account the total number of eligible children in the household:

Total monthly = (Children 1-2 × basic rate) + (Children 3+ × increased rate)

*Note: Fribourg uses the "only_from3" rule to better support large families.

3

One-time allowances (Birth / Adoption)

In the canton of Fribourg, you are entitled to a one-time payment upon the birth or adoption of a child:

Allowance type Amount (FR)
Birth allowance CHF 1'500
Adoption allowance CHF 1'500
4

Annual entitlement calculation

The annual total includes monthly benefits as well as one-time allowances:

Annual amount = (Monthly total × 12) + One-time allowances
Important information for FR:
Fribourg is one of the few cantons that pays birth allowances. Make sure to claim them within the statutory deadline through your employer or directly via ECASFR.
