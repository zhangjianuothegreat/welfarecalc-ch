This document discloses the official calculation algorithm for determining emergency aid ( Aide d'urgence ) in the canton of Neuchâtel. The transparency of our algorithms is the foundation of trustworthy and independent social counselling at welfarecalc.ch .

1. Legal Parameters (as of 2026)

Unlike cantons such as Aargau or Appenzell Innerrhoden that apply daily degression rates, the canton of Neuchâtel establishes a fixed monthly lump sum per present, eligible person.

Monthly Lump Sum (per capita)

CHF 320.00

Calculation Basis

Individual / Equal treatment

Cantonal Guideline

ANCAM Art. 5 Para. 2

Accommodation & Benefits in kind

Collective (provided separately)

2. The Calculation Algorithm Step by Step

1

Household size determination

The exact number of persons living in the same household (or collective accommodation) who meet the criteria for receiving emergency aid under cantonal law is identified. In Neuchâtel's algorithm, there is no mathematical distinction between adults and children regarding the basic emergency aid entitlement.

2

Application of the legal monthly lump sum

The total monthly entitlement is derived directly from multiplying the number of eligible persons by the legally defined lump sum amount.

Monthly amount = (Number of adults + Number of children) × CHF 320.00
3

Time scaling (Day / Year)

For temporary or sub-monthly settlements, the system uses the official Swiss average month of 30.4167 days (365 days / 12 months).

Metric Calculation formula
Daily equivalent Monthly amount / 30.42 (commercially rounded)
Annual equivalent Monthly amount × 12
Legal notice & Source: According to the cantonal decree or the Arrêté fixant les normes pour le calcul de l'aide matérielle (ANCAM - RSN 831.02) of November 4, 1998 (version 2026) Art. 5 paragraph 2 , the emergency aid lump sum is exactly CHF 320.00 per month . Benefits in kind for accommodation and basic medical care are handled directly by the competent cantonal authorities.
