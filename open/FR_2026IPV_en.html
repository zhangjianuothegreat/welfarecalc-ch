Radical transparency: Here you will learn exactly how our calculator determines the 2026 premium reduction (IPV) for the Canton of Fribourg. The algorithm uses the official 60-level smoothing model from ECASFR.

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Income Threshold (Starting point)

CHF 37,000 (single person)

Basis for determining the entitlement level.

Wealth Surcharge

5.0 %

Proportion of net assets added to income.

2. Calculation Steps

1

Relevant Income (LNA)

The LNA is calculated from your tax data as follows:

[Net income] + [5% of net assets] - [Child deduction: CHF 5,000 per child]

Additionally, paid alimony or maintenance contributions are deducted.

2

Reference Premiums (Monthly)

The Canton of Fribourg uses the following flat-rate amounts for 2026:

CategoryAmount per month
Adults (26 years and older)CHF 495 / month
Young adults (19-25 years)CHF 415 / month
Children (0-18 years)CHF 136 / month
3

Smoothing Model (60 Levels / Paliers)

Fribourg uses linear smoothing. First, the percentage distance to the individual income threshold is determined:

Distance % = [(Threshold amount - LNA) / Threshold amount] × 100

Based on this distance, the subsidy rate (1% to 65%) is linearly interpolated across 60 levels.

4

Minimum Guarantee for Dependents

Regardless of the calculated rate, the Canton guarantees:

CategoryGuaranteed Rate
Childrenmin. 80 %
Youths in education/trainingmin. 50 %
Exclusion criteria:
If net assets exceed CHF 250,000 or net income exceeds CHF 150,000, there is generally no entitlement to IPV in the Canton of Fribourg.
