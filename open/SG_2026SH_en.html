How is social assistance calculated in the Canton of St. Gallen? To strengthen the trust of our users, we disclose the full calculation algorithm behind welfarecalc.ch. It is based on the St. Gallen Social Assistance Act (SHG) as well as the official SKOS guidelines 2026.

1. Asset Check (The Entry Threshold)

Allowance for single person

CHF 6'000

Plus CHF 3'000 per child or person in education/training.

Allowance for married couples

CHF 12'000

Maximum total allowance capped at CHF 15'000 per assistance unit.

2. Calculation Method (Monthly)

1

Basic Living Expenses (GBL)

The GBL covers costs for food, clothing, energy, and household needs:

Household size Amount per month (2026)
1 person CHF 1'061
2 persons CHF 1'624
3 persons CHF 1'974
4 persons CHF 2'271
5 persons CHF 2'568
Each additional person + CHF 216

*Special feature SG: For young adults living in their own housing unit or in training, specific SKOS gradations apply (e.g., CHF 400 for other adults in the same household).

2

Housing Costs & Healthcare (Net Principle)

Recognition of actual housing and health insurance costs up to the cantonal or municipal ceiling:

Recognized housing costs = Min(Actual rent, Municipal rent limit)
Net health insurance = Actual premium (KVG) - Individual premium reduction (IPV)

*Note: In the Canton of SG, maximum rent limits vary by municipality (benchmark for 1 person in the middle segment is approx. CHF 1'100 gross). IPV is directly offset against the premium.

3

Income Deduction & Employment Allowance (EFB)

An income allowance is deducted from net earned income to maintain work incentives:

Countable income = Net income - EFB (max. CHF 400 per person)

Other income such as family allowances, maintenance contributions (alimony), or pensions are counted as 100% income.

4

Result: Monthly financial entitlement

The difference calculation determines the monthly support amount:

Entitlement = (GBL + Recognized rent + Net health insurance + IZA*) - (Countable income)

*IZA = Integration allowance for documented integration efforts or employment (benchmark: CHF 100 per employed person).

Important note on assets & recovery:
If liquid assets at the initial review exceed the allowance, there is no entitlement to social assistance under the law. Non-liquid assets (e.g., real estate) may lead to a collateral pledge by way of a land charge or a contractual obligation to repay.
