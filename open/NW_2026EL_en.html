2. Detailed Calculation Path

1

Recognized Expenses (Needs)

First, the algorithm adds up all legally recognized expense items:

Basic needs + Recognized rent (up to limit) + Health insurance premium (capped)

2

Assessable Income

Then all assessable income is offset:

  • Pensions & Income: Regular AHV/IV pensions. Legal neutralization: The 13th AHV pension (approved by public vote) is completely EL-neutral and is deducted (AHV income / 13 * 12).
  • Earned income: 2/3 of the amount after deducting the allowance (CHF 1'300 for singles / CHF 1'950 for married couples).
  • Asset depletion: max(0, (Taxable net assets - Allowance) / Divisor). The divisor is 10 for old-age pensioners and 15 for IV (disability insurance) beneficiaries.

3

Result: Legal EL Entitlement

The annual supplementary benefit exactly closes the gap between needs and income:

Annual EL = max(0, [Recognized Expenses] - [Assessable Income])

The monthly payment amount is rounded up to the next whole franc.

Important: The Asset Threshold (Entry Threshold)
A legal entitlement to EL exists only if the taxable net assets (excluding the value of owner-occupied residential property) are below the entry threshold:
  • Single persons: CHF 100'000
  • Married couples: CHF 200'000
  • Additional per child: + CHF 50'000
