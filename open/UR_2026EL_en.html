Transparency creates trust. Our calculation model for the canton of Uri is strictly based on the binding provisions of the Federal Act on Supplementary Benefits (ELG 2026) and the cantonal implementing regulations of SVS Uri.

1. Annual Basic Needs (Statutory Adjustment 2026)

Single Persons

CHF 21'510

Nationally adjusted amount for general living costs from 2026.

Married Couples

CHF 32'265

Basic needs for spouses living together in one household.

2. The Official Calculation Path

1

Recognized Expenditure (Needs)

First, the algorithm determines the sum of the legally recognized expenditure items per year:

Total_Expenditure = Basic_Needs + Recognized_Rent + Recognized_Health_Insurance_Premium
Rent Maxima (Region 3 - Entire Canton Uri) Amount p.a. (excl. ancillary costs)
1-person household CHF 16'680 (CHF 1'390/month)
2-person household CHF 20'160 (CHF 1'680/month)
3-person household CHF 22'200 (CHF 1'850/month)

*Note: Plus ancillary cost flat rate according to federal law. For homeowners, an ancillary cost flat rate of CHF 3'480 p.a. is credited. A wheelchair supplement increases the limit by CHF 6'900 p.a.

Health Insurance Premium Logic (ELG Art. 11)

The algorithm checks the applicant's effective premium:

Recognized_Health_Insurance_Premium = Math.min(Effective_Premium, Cantonal_Average_Premium)

*Cantonal standard rate UR 2026 as maximum upper limit: Adults CHF 5'850/year, Young Adults CHF 4'195/year, Children CHF 1'337/year.

2

Attributable Income

Subsequently, all income and assets are precisely compared:

  • Pension Income (13th AHV Pension Neutralization): The newly introduced 13th AHV pension in 2026 is mathematically excluded to protect the EL entitlement. The income basis remains the 12-month pension.
  • Earned Income: Adjusted for allowances (CHF 1'300 single persons / CHF 1'950 married couples). Only 2/3 of the remaining amount is counted as income.
  • Asset Consumption (Living at Home):
    (Net assets - Asset allowance) / Divisor
    Divisor = 10 for old-age pensioners (AHV) | Divisor = 15 for disability pensioners (IV).
EL Allowances (Assets) Allowance
Single Persons CHF 30'000
Married Couples CHF 50'000
Additional per child CHF 15'000
Owner-occupied property (basic) CHF 112'500
Owner-occupied property (partner in care home) CHF 300'000
3

Result: EL Benefit

If there is a financial coverage gap and the statutory thresholds are met, the annual EL pension results:

Annual EL Benefit = Max(0, Total_Expenditure - Attributable_Income)

The monthly payout corresponds to the annual benefit divided by 12, rounded commercially to the nearest franc.

Important: The statutory entry threshold (Asset limit)
A statutory entitlement to EL is generally excluded if the net assets (without considering the value of the owner-occupied property) exceed the following thresholds:
• CHF 100'000 for single persons
• CHF 200'000 for married couples (plus CHF 50'000 per child).
