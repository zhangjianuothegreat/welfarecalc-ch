Transparency builds trust. Supplementary Benefits (EL) to AHV/IV guarantee the constitutional minimum subsistence level. Here we document precisely how our core calculator determines your entitlement in the canton of Valais based on federal legislation (ELG 2026) and the cantonal implementation guidelines of the CCVS.

1. Statutory Entry Threshold (Asset Check)

Before a mathematical entitlement is determined, a statutory blocking check (exclusion criterion) is carried out. If the taxable net assets exceed the statutory threshold, there is no entitlement to EL.

Single Persons

CHF 100'000

Hard asset limit for individuals.

Married Couples

CHF 200'000

Hard asset limit for married couples.

*Note: The value of an owner-occupied property is not counted towards this primary entry threshold.

2. The Calculation Path in Detail

1

Recognized Expenses (Needs)

First, the system sums up all legally recognized expense items:

Recognized Expenses = Basic Needs + Rent (up to limit) + Health Insurance Flat Rate

A) Federal Cost of Living Basic Needs

Nationwide flat rate from January 2026:

  • Single Persons: CHF 20'670 p.a.
  • Married Couples: CHF 31'005 p.a.

B) Regional Maximum Rent Limits in Canton Valais (2026)

The canton of Valais divides municipalities into Region 2 (centers) and Region 3 (rural). Our calculator assigns the limits based on your postal code:

Household Size Region 2 (Centers)* Region 3 (Other Municipalities)**
1 Person CHF 18'300 (CHF 1'525/Month) CHF 16'680 (CHF 1'390/Month)
2 Persons CHF 21'720 (CHF 1'810/Month) CHF 20'160 (CHF 1'680/Month)
3 Persons CHF 23'760 (CHF 1'980/Month) CHF 22'200 (CHF 1'850/Month)
* Region 2 includes Valais centers such as Sion, Martigny, Monthey, Visp, Brig-Glis.
** Region 3 includes all other Valais municipalities (mountain areas and rural areas).

C) Health Insurance Premium Flat Rate Valais 2026

It is not the actual premium that is taken into account, but the cantonal average premium (flat rate according to WEL):

  • Adults (26 years and older): CHF 6'732 p.a.
  • Young Adults (18–25 years): CHF 4'988 p.a.
  • Children (up to 17 years): CHF 1'629 p.a.
2

Offsettable Income

The modified income is offset against the needs:

  • Pensions & 13th AHV Pension: Regular pensions are offset at 100%. The 13th AHV pension paid out since 2026 is legally fully neutralized (mathematically excluded in the algorithm and remains unconsidered).
  • Earned Income: Adjusted by the statutory allowance (CHF 1'300 single / CHF 1'950 married couples). Only 2/3 of the remaining amount is recorded as income.
  • Asset Consumption: If the net assets exceed the allowance (CHF 30'000 single / CHF 50'000 couples), the portion exceeding the allowance is counted as annual income at 1/10 (for IV pensioners at 1/15).
Property Allowance (owner-occupied) Amount
Standard allowance for owners CHF 112'500
Increased allowance (e.g., upon partner's admission to a nursing home) CHF 300'000
3

Result: Calculation of the EL Pension

If there is a coverage gap between recognized expenses and income, this shortfall is paid out as an annual supplementary benefit:

Annual EL Pension = [Recognized Expenses] - [Offsettable Income]

The monthly payment corresponds to the annual amount divided by 12, rounded commercially to the nearest franc.
