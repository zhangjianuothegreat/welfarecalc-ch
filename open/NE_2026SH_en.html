Transparency is the foundation of trust. On this page, we disclose the complete algorithm that drives the calculations for the Canton of Neuchâtel (Neuchâtel) on welfarecalc.ch. The logic is based on cantonal legislation (LASoc) as well as the SKOS guidelines 2026.

1. Asset Check (The Initial Threshold)

Allowance for Single Person

CHF 6'000

Plus CHF 3'000 per child or person in education/training.

Allowance for Couples

CHF 12'000

Maximum total allowance capped at CHF 15'000 per household.

2. Calculation Method (Monthly)

1

Basic Living Expenses (BLE)

The BLE 2026 in Canton NE incorporates the SKOS cost-of-living adjustment:

Household Size Amount per Month (2026)
1 Person CHF 1,061
2 Persons CHF 1,624
Additional Adult CHF 400
Child under 11 years CHF 391
Child 11 - 15 years CHF 699
Child over 15 / in education CHF 1,061
2

Housing Costs & Health Insurance

Actual costs are considered up to the cantonal maximum. In Canton NE, the IPV (individual premium reduction) principle takes precedence:

Recognized Need = Rent (up to max.) + (Health Insurance Premium - IPV Contribution)

*Note: Our calculator uses cantonal rent limits (e.g., approx. CHF 1,200 for 1 person).

3

Income Assessment & Work-Related Deduction

To promote professional integration, part of the income is not counted:

Countable Income = Net earnings - Work-related deduction (20%)

Integration allowances (IZU) of CHF 100/month are additionally granted for gainful employment.

4

Result: Monthly Entitlement

Coverage of needs is determined by the difference calculation:

Entitlement = Total Need - (Income + Asset Depletion*)

*Excess assets are counted using a divisor of 120 months (10 years).

Special feature in Canton NE:
The individual premium reduction (IPV) is directly integrated into the calculation in the Canton of Neuchâtel. Only the remaining health insurance costs are recognized as need in the budget.
