In Switzerland, family allowances are regulated by the federal law (FamZG). Our simulator uses the rates for 2026 legally anchored in the Canton of Solothurn to precisely calculate your potential entitlement. Algorithm transparency builds trust.

1. Monthly Rates SO (2026)

Child Allowance

CHF 215 / Month

Valid for children from the month of birth until the completion of the 16th year of age.

Training Allowance

CHF 268 / Month

From the 16th year of age until the end of training (maximum until the 25th year of age).

2. The Calculation Path

1

Determination of the Category

The calculation core checks the age and training status of each child separately:

  • 0 - 16 years: Full entitlement to child allowance (CHF 215).
  • 16 - 25 years: Entitlement to training allowance (CHF 268), provided recognized training is proven.
  • Disabled children: Entitlement to child allowance until the completion of the 20th year of age.
2

Calculation of the Monthly Amount

The Canton of Solothurn applies a linear calculation model without progressive scaling based on the number of children:

(Number of children × CHF 215) + (Number in training × CHF 268)

*Note: In Canton SO, the federal minimum amounts according to FamZG apply without cantonal supplements.

3

One-time Allowances (Birth/Adoption)

The Canton of Solothurn does not grant any one-time birth or adoption allowances under cantonal law.

Allowance Type Amount (SO)
Birth Allowance CHF 0
Adoption Allowance CHF 0
4

Determination of the Annual Entitlement

Payment is made monthly via the employer or the responsible compensation office:

Annual Amount = (Monthly Total Entitlement) × 12
Important Note on Entitlement Competition:
Only one allowance can be claimed per child. If both parents are employed, the primary entitlement is determined strictly according to the legal priority order pursuant to Art. 12 FamZG.
