Trust through transparency. Here we disclose how our universal calculator determines your potential premium reduction (IPV) in the canton of Uri. This mathematical algorithm is strictly based on the legal requirements of the Social Insurance Office Uri (SVS Uri).

1. Core Parameters (2026)

Personal Deductible

8.5 %

Reduced from 9.75%. The household's own contribution to the PV income.

Asset Surcharge Rate

15.0 %

Fixed percentage of taxable assets added to income.

2. The Calculation Path

1

Relevant PV Income

The economic capacity of the household is based on cantonal tax data, expanded by a hypothetical asset inflow:

PV_Income = [Net Income] + ([Taxable Assets] × 15%) - [Alimony Paid]
2

Cantonal Reference Premiums 2026

The Government Council of the Canton of Uri defines the standardized target premium volume per year as follows:

Age / Person Group Statutory Reference Premium (CHF / Year)
Adults (from 26 years and 19–25 years without education) CHF 4'368
Young Adults (19–25 years, in education) CHF 2'844
Children (up to 18 years) CHF 1'104
Total_Reference_Premium = (Number_Adults × 4'368) + (Number_Young_Adults_in_Education × 2'844) + (Number_Children × 1'104)
3

Deductible & Social Minimum Guarantee

First, the ordinary household deductible is calculated:

Household_Deductible = PV_Income × 8.5%

If the determined PV_Income is at or below **CHF 90'000**, the cantonal **Minimum Guarantee** applies to protect families:

Category (for PV Income ≤ CHF 90'000) Guaranteed Minimum Amount
Children (up to 18 years) 80% of the reference premium (= CHF 883.20 / year)
Young Adults in Education (19-25) 50% of the reference premium (= CHF 1'422.00 / year)
Guaranteed_Minimum_Amount = (Number_Children × 883.20) + (Number_Young_Adults_in_Education × 1'422.00)
4

Final Entitlement

The calculation engine determines the ordinary entitlement and dynamically compares it:

Ordinary_Entitlement = Total_Reference_Premium - Household_Deductible
Final_Entitlement = Max(Ordinary_Entitlement, Guaranteed_Minimum_Amount)

The final IPV entitlement is capped by the effectively paid household premium and rounded commercially to whole francs.

Important note on asset testing:
In contrast to models with rigid asset limits, the Canton of Uri uses a fluid system. Extremely high asset values automatically lead to a high deductible via the 15% surcharge in the first step, whereby a potential entitlement is mathematically precisely and socially fairly eliminated.
