In Switzerland, family allowances are regulated by law (FamZG). Our calculator uses the specific cantonal rates of the Canton of Thurgau for 2026 to precisely determine your entitlement. Transparency builds trust.

1. Monthly Rates (2026)

Child Allowance

CHF 215 / Month

For children from 0 up to the age of 16. (Daily rate for partial months: CHF 7.20)

Training Allowance

CHF 280 / Month

From age 16 until the end of education (max. 25). (Daily rate for partial months: CHF 9.35)

2. The Calculation Path

1

Determination of the Category

The algorithm checks the age and status of each child:

  • 0 - 16 years: Entitled to child allowance.
  • 16 - 25 years: Entitled to training allowance (proof of education required).
  • Disabled children: Entitled to child allowance up to the age of 20.
2

Calculation of the Monthly Amount

The total is formed linearly from the respective individual rates:

Monthly Total Amount = (Number of children < 16 × CHF 215) + (Number in training × CHF 280)

*Note: In the Canton of Thurgau, there is no tiering or increase for large families (from the 3rd child onwards), as is common in some other French-speaking cantons.

3

One-time Allowances (Birth/Adoption)

The Canton of Thurgau grants no one-time birth or adoption allowances under its law (TG FamZV).

Allowance Type Amount in Canton Thurgau (TG)
Birth Allowance CHF 0
Adoption Allowance CHF 0
4

Determination of the Annual Entitlement

Payment is made standardly via the employer or the cantonal family compensation fund:

Annual Amount = (Monthly Total Entitlement) × 12
Entitlement & Regulation:
The Federal Act on Family Allowances (FamZG) applies. Only one parent can receive the allowance. If both parents are employed, the statutory priority order (Art. 12 FamZG) governs the primary entitlement. Additionally, the minimum AHV income must be reached.
