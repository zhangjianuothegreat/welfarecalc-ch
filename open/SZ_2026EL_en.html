Transparency builds trust. Supplementary benefits (EL) to AHV/IV secure the constitutional minimum subsistence level. Here we document completely and mathematically precisely how our calculator determines your entitlement in the canton of Schwyz according to federal specifications (ELG) and cantonal guidelines of the SVA SZ.

Step 0: Statutory Asset Threshold (Entry Check)
A legal entitlement to EL exists after the reform only if the net assets are below CHF 100,000 (single persons) or CHF 200,000 (married couples). The value of an owner-occupied property is not counted towards this entry threshold.

1. Annual Basic Needs (2026)

Single Persons

CHF 20,670

Nationally uniform flat rate for living expenses.

Married Couples

CHF 31,005

Combined statutory basic needs for both spouses.

2. The Official Calculation Method

1

Recognized Expenditure (Requirements)

First, all legally recognized annual expenditure items are summed up:

Recognized Expenditure = Basic Needs + Recognized Gross Rent + Health Insurance Benchmark Premium

Recognized Maximum Amounts for Rent (SVA SZ 2026)

In the canton of Schwyz, the limits depend on the residential region (Region 2 e.g. Schwyz, Freienbach, Einsiedeln or Region 3 e.g. rural municipalities):

Household Size Region 2 (p.a. / p.m.) Region 3 (p.a. / p.m.)
1 Person CHF 18,300 (CHF 1,525) CHF 16,680 (CHF 1,390)
2 Persons CHF 21,720 (CHF 1,810) CHF 20,160 (CHF 1,680)
3 Persons CHF 23,760 (CHF 1,980) CHF 22,200 (CHF 1,850)

Per-Capita Principle: If the entitled person lives in a shared apartment (WG) or together with persons not entitled to EL, the actual rental costs are first divided proportionally per capita and then compared with the respective maximum.

Health Insurance Flat-Rate Premiums (SVA SZ 2026)

Not the actual premium is credited, but the average premium set by the canton:

  • Adults: CHF 6,204 / year
  • Young Adults (11–25 years): CHF 4,368 / year
  • Children (0–10 years): CHF 1,428 / year
2

Creditable Income

The creditable income is mathematically precisely compared to the expenditure:

Types of Income in the Algorithm:

  • Pensions and Daily Allowances: AHV or IV pensions are credited at 100% (The 13th AHV pension introduced in 2026 is legally neutralized and does not flow into the EL calculation).
  • Earned Income: Gross earned income minus social insurance contributions. An annual allowance of CHF 1,300 (single persons) or CHF 1,950 (married couples) applies. Only 2/3 of the remaining amount is credited as income.
  • Asset Income: Actual income such as bank interest or dividends.
  • Asset Consumption: (Net assets + hypothetical waived assets - allowance) / consumption divisor.
Allowances & Divisors (Assets) Value / Regulation
Asset allowance (Single persons) CHF 30,000
Asset allowance (Married couples) CHF 50,000
Allowance for owner-occupied property CHF 112,500 (Under certain circumstances CHF 300,000)
Consumption divisor (Old-age pension / AHV) 10 (10% of creditable assets per year)
Consumption divisor (Disability pension / IV) 15 (6.67% of creditable assets per year)

Waived Assets: Assets that were given away without legal obligation or adequate consideration (e.g., advance inheritance) are fully credited, whereby this value is amortized annually by CHF 10,000 from the following year.

3

Result: Calculation of the EL Pension

The annual supplementary benefit exactly closes the calculated gap:

Annual EL Pension = Max(0, [Recognized Expenditure] - [Creditable Income])

The monthly paid EL pension corresponds exactly to the annual entitlement divided by 12.
