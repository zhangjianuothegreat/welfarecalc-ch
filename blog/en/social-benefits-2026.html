Why You Might Be Eligible for More Than You Think

Switzerland is known for its well-developed social system. However, what many don't realize: approximately 40% of eligible individuals do not claim their benefits – due to lack of awareness, embarrassment, or fear that the procedures are too complicated.

Those particularly affected include:

  • 👥 Migrants with B or C permits
  • 👩‍👧 Part-time workers and single parents
  • 👴 Retirees with modest incomes
  • 🩺 People with physical or mental limitations

This guide explains step by step what benefits are available and how to check your eligibility simply, for free, and anonymously.

1. 👴 AHV/AVS Pension – The Foundation for Retirement

The Old Age and Survivors' Insurance (AHV/AVS) forms the first pillar of the Swiss social security system. It aims to cover basic living needs during retirement.

Key Points:

  • Entitlement begins at the ordinary retirement age (64/65 years)
  • The amount depends on contribution years and average annual income
  • A full AHV/AVS pension is paid after 44 contribution years (women) or 45 years (men)

💡 Tip: Even if you haven't worked continuously, educational and caregiving credits can increase your pension.

2. 🩺 IV/AI Disability Insurance – In Case of Illness or Disability

Disability Insurance (IV/AI) steps in when you are unable to work, or can only work partially, due to health reasons.

Key Criteria:

  • The degree of disability must be at least 40%
  • The IV/AI assesses whether medical measures or professional reintegration can improve the situation
  • Children with disabilities are also entitled to a child's disability pension

📌 Note: An IV/AI process can take several months. Early assessment is therefore strongly recommended.

3. 💰 Supplementary Benefits (EL/PC) – The Overlooked Assistance

Supplementary benefits to AHV/AVS and IV/AI (EL/PC) are the most important social benefit, but they are too often overlooked.

What You Should Know:

  • Supplementary benefits are paid when the AHV/AVS or IV/AI pension and income do not cover basic living expenses
  • There is no repayment obligation – unlike social welfare
  • Supplementary benefits also cover health insurance premiums, dental treatments, and nursing home costs

👉 For retirees with small pensions and people with disabilities, supplementary benefits are often the deciding factor for a dignified life.

4. 🏥 Premium Reduction (IPV/RIP) – Save on Health Insurance

The individual premium reduction (IPV/RIP) for health insurance is well-known, but not all eligible individuals apply for it.

Key Points:

  • It reduces monthly health insurance premiums
  • Applications are submitted to your canton of residence – deadlines vary significantly!
  • The determining factors are income and assets, not marital status

⚠️ Important: In some cantons, eligibility expires automatically if you don't take action! Many cantons set application deadlines as early as September or October. Those who miss the deadline must wait an entire year for the reduction.

5. 💼 Unemployment Benefits – Protection in Case of Job Loss

Unemployment insurance protects you in case of involuntary job loss.

Requirements:

  • You must have contributed for at least 12 months in the last 2 years
  • You must reside in Switzerland and be registered as a job seeker
  • The amount of daily allowances depends on your insured salary

📌 Important: Depending on the canton and age, different framework periods apply. Timely registration with the RAV (Regional Employment Center) is crucial.

6. 👶 Family Allowances – Even for Non-Employed Parents?

Child and education allowances are generally available to all parents – even if you are not employed.

Current Values (2026):

  • Child allowance: approximately CHF 200–300 per child per month (varies by canton)
  • Education allowance: until the end of initial education (maximum 25 years)

💡 Many self-employed and non-employed parents forget to claim these allowances.

7. 🏠 Social Welfare – The Last Resort

When all other benefits are insufficient, social welfare can step in. However, it is subject to strict conditions and may affect residency status.

Our Tip: Check all other benefits such as supplementary benefits, disability insurance, and premium reductions before applying for social welfare.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Question: Am I eligible as a foreigner with a B permit?
Yes – most social benefits are available to all individuals residing in Switzerland, regardless of residency status. Restrictions only apply to social welfare. Important: Supplementary benefits (EL/PC) and premium reductions (IPV/RIP) are not considered social welfare and do not affect your residency status.
Question: Are premium reductions paid automatically?
No. You must apply every year to your canton. WelfareCalc shows you whether it's worth the effort.
Question: Can I apply for supplementary benefits if I already receive AHV/AVS?
Yes, at any time. Supplementary benefits are granted retroactively for the current year – a timely check is therefore recommended.
